# B-Cell Neoantigen Proposal Anchor

Project-side anchor for a research proposal building a proteogenomics-based B-cell neoantigen discovery and interpretation pipeline across human cancers.

## Key Points

- The proposal frames current neoantigen analysis as too T-cell-centric and positions B-cell or antibody-facing neoantigen interpretation as an underbuilt layer in cancer immunology.
- Its core plan is to integrate genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, and public single-cell or immune-context resources to prioritize B-cell-relevant neoantigen candidates across multiple cancers.
- The project specifically links neoantigen discovery to B-cell infiltration, tertiary lymphoid structures, and tumor microenvironment organization, making immune context part of target prioritization rather than a downstream afterthought.
- The proposal is method-heavy and pipeline-oriented, so future papers on proteogenomics, noncanonical antigens, B-cell epitope prediction, TLS biology, and immune deconvolution should be connectable back to this page.
- This page should function as the proposal-side anchor for relevance scoring inside the B-cell neoantigen topic map.

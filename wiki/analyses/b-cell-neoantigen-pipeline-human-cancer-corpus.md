# B-Cell Neoantigen Pipeline in Human Cancer

This corpus maps two adjacent but complementary literatures: neoantigen discovery and therapeutic deployment on one side, and B-cell/TLS-mediated intratumoral immune organization on the other.

## Summary

The direct literature on a single named "B-cell neoantigen pipeline" in human cancer is still sparse. The most useful working interpretation is therefore a combined pipeline:

1. Discover and prioritize high-quality neoantigens.
2. Validate presentation, immunogenicity, and therapeutic tractability.
3. Interpret likely efficacy in the context of B-cell states, TLS maturity, and local immune architecture.

## Key Takeaways

- Neoantigen work is strongest upstream: candidate generation, presentation evidence, and therapeutic translation.
- B-cell/TLS work is strongest downstream: whether the tumor microenvironment can organize durable local antitumor immunity.
- A practical translational pipeline should not treat these as separate silos.
- Mature TLS and competent B-cell programs may help explain why similarly designed neoantigen interventions perform differently across tumors.

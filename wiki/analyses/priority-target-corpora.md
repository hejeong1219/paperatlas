# Priority Target Corpora

Working acquisition queue for the next wave of high-impact, human-cancer-centered papers that should strengthen the three active seed projects.

## Key Points

- This page translates the anchor keyword clusters into concrete acquisition priorities.
- It is intentionally biased toward recent human cohort, biopsy, clinical trial, ctDNA, spatial, and single-cell studies in high-impact venues.
- It excludes mouse or murine models, xenograft or PDX work, organoid-only studies, and low-priority venues already filtered elsewhere.

## Resistance Manuscript Priority Targets

- Human checkpoint resistance papers with paired pre-treatment and post-progression biopsies.
- Human studies of HLA loss, B2M loss, JAK loss, and interferon pathway dysfunction under PD-1 or PD-L1 therapy.
- Human translational studies linking EGFR, KRAS, STK11, KEAP1, or MET states to immune-cold or immune-excluded phenotypes.
- Human endocrine-resistance papers with immune microenvironment readouts, especially ESR1-related or CDK4/6-related immune-state work.
- Human PARP-resistance studies that connect DNA-repair escape to antigen presentation, STING, or checkpoint response.
- Human TLS, stromal exclusion, fibroblast, macrophage, and vascular barrier studies tied to clinical immunotherapy outcomes.

## B-Cell Neoantigen Proposal Priority Targets

- Human proteogenomic or immunopeptidomic studies that identify neoantigens directly from tumor samples.
- Human studies linking neoantigens to B-cell infiltration, antibody responses, or tertiary lymphoid structures.
- Human spatial transcriptomic and single-cell studies of TLS-rich tumors in lung, pancreas, stomach, cervix, ovary, and related cancers.
- Human personalized or shared neoantigen vaccine studies with immune monitoring beyond bulk response rate reporting.
- Human papers on public neoantigens and shared neoepitope-HLA pairs with translational validation in patient-derived material.
- Human studies that connect noncanonical peptides or proteogenomic prioritization to clinically relevant tumor immune context.

## ptmanchor Manuscript Priority Targets

- Human cancer phosphoproteomic cohort papers with matched protein and phosphosite measurements.
- Human CPTAC-scale or pan-cancer proteomics studies where signaling inference depends on abundance-aware normalization.
- Human proteogenomic studies that compare kinase or pathway inference across tumors and cohorts.
- Human papers on protein-adjusted phosphosite interpretation, site-level normalization, or reproducibility of PTM signals across clinical cohorts.
- Human cancer studies where PTM-aware analysis changes biological interpretation relative to bulk protein abundance alone.

# Anchor Progress Baseline

Working baseline for how many ingested source pages are explicitly linked around each active seed project, with per-anchor growth now tracked separately rather than through a single global PDF total.

## Key Points

- The current corpus is still highly imbalanced toward the resistance manuscript.
- The B-cell neoantigen proposal and ptmanchor manuscript are now being tracked by explicit wiki linkage, which gives a more honest per-anchor minimum count than the earlier heuristic estimate.
- The numbers below reflect source pages that are currently linked to the relevant anchor or topic pages inside `wiki/`.
- This page is still a planning baseline rather than a final classification truth table.

## Current Baseline

- Resistance manuscript: about `50` source pages are explicitly linked to the resistance anchor or resistance topic.
- B-cell neoantigen proposal: `18` source pages are explicitly linked to the B-cell anchor or B-cell topic.
- ptmanchor manuscript: `11` source pages are explicitly linked to the ptmanchor anchor or PTM topic.

## Gap To Target

- Resistance manuscript: not the immediate target for new acquisition under the current workflow.
- B-cell neoantigen proposal: needs roughly `32` more linked papers to reach `50`.
- ptmanchor manuscript: needs roughly `39` more linked papers to reach `50`.

## Operational Meaning

- Resistance can keep growing in the background, but it is no longer the acquisition goal.
- The immediate acquisition pressure stays on B-cell neoantigen and PTM-aware cancer proteomics papers that satisfy the strict human-cancer and venue filters.
- New downloads should continue to be linked explicitly at ingest time so growth is measured per anchor instead of through one undifferentiated global total.

# Soluble and Metabolic Mediators of Immunoresistance

Not all resistance is encoded as a fixed genomic lesion. Tumors can also create suppressive fields through soluble cytokines, circulating vesicles, and metabolic products that blunt immune-cell trafficking, activation, or persistence.

## Key Points

- Lactate and acidosis can suppress effector lymphocytes while reinforcing suppressive myeloid and regulatory programs.
- Exosomal PD-L1 extends checkpoint biology beyond direct tumor-cell contact and suggests that circulating immune suppression can track treatment response or adaptive resistance.
- GDF-15 represents a clinically actionable soluble mediator that may restrict immune-cell entry and create PD-1 or PD-L1 refractory states.
- These mediators matter because they can preserve an apparently intact tumor genome while still making the microenvironment therapeutically non-productive.
- This axis sits between tumor-intrinsic and host-context explanations of resistance and often creates a rationale for combination therapy.

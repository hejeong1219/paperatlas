# CDK4/6 Inhibition, Senescence, and Tumor Immunogenicity

CDK4/6 inhibition is not only a cell-cycle intervention. It can create a therapy-induced cell state whose immune consequences vary, making senescence quality as important as senescence quantity.

## Key Points

- CDK4/6 inhibitors can induce quiescence or senescence depending on cellular context.
- Senescence induced by CDK4/6 blockade may be less pro-tumorigenic and more immune-supportive than senescence induced by DNA-damaging therapy.
- Antigen-presentation programs and T-cell-attracting chemokines can remain relatively preserved under CDK4/6 inhibitor-induced senescence.
- Senolytic or state-directed follow-up strategies may matter because senescent cells are not biologically neutral.
- This axis connects targeted therapy, cell state, and immune modulation in endocrine-responsive breast cancer.

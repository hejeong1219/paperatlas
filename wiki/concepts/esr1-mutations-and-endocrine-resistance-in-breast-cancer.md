# ESR1 Mutations and Endocrine Resistance in Breast Cancer

`ESR1` mutation is one of the clearest examples of therapy-shaped molecular evolution in metastatic breast cancer. The receptor remains the disease's organizing axis, but ligand-binding-domain mutations let it keep signaling when estrogen deprivation or standard endocrine therapy should have shut it down.

## Key Points

- Recurrent ligand-binding-domain mutations such as `Y537S`, `Y537N`, and `D538G` drive ligand-independent ER activity.
- ESR1 mutation is a clinically actionable resistance event rather than just a retrospective marker.
- ctDNA monitoring can detect ESR1 molecular escape before overt progression and support adaptive treatment switching.
- Later work suggests ESR1-mutant disease may also alter immune surveillance, extending the consequences of endocrine resistance beyond ER signaling alone.
- This axis links mutation discovery, liquid-biopsy monitoring, and therapy adaptation.

# ADC Target Expression and HER2-Dependent Resistance

HER2-directed ADCs make it clear that response is not governed by payload potency alone. Clinical benefit depends on whether sufficient target remains available for binding, internalization, and intratumoral payload delivery over time.

## Key Points

- DESTINY-Breast04 showed that even HER2-low disease can benefit from trastuzumab deruxtecan, proving that modest target abundance can still be therapeutically relevant.
- DESTINY-Breast06 pushed benefit into earlier-line HER2-low and HER2-ultralow disease, further weakening any rigid baseline HER2 threshold model.
- The resistance paper by Chen et al. shows that progression can still occur through HER2 downregulation, complete HER2 loss, or mutations in the trastuzumab binding interface.
- This means ADC resistance is often a delivery problem rather than a pure payload problem: tumor cells may remain sensitive to deruxtecan if the drug can be delivered through another antigen.
- A practical implication is that serial HER2 measurement, spatial heterogeneity, and multi-target ADC combinations may matter more than a single archival HER2 assay.
- T-DXd can also trigger interferon-related and cGAS-STING-linked immune programs, so its biology is not limited to payload delivery alone.

# Gut Microbiota and Immunoresistance

The gut microbiota is best understood here as a host-level immune context setter: it can prime or blunt systemic antitumor immunity before tumor-specific or therapy-specific mechanisms even enter the picture.

## Key Points

- Response to checkpoint blockade is shaped not only by tumor genomics and the local microenvironment but also by microbial ecology in the gut.
- Dysbiosis can be treated as a resistance-prone state rather than as a vague background variable.
- The field is moving from retrospective association studies toward intervention logic, including microbial consortia, fecal microbiota transplantation, and host-factor management.
- Microbiota-linked effects are mechanistically heterogeneous and may influence efficacy and toxicity simultaneously.
- This axis complements tumor-focused biomarker models such as TMB, GEP, and TLS rather than replacing them.

# MSI-H and dMMR Heterogeneity in Immunotherapy Response

MSI-high and mismatch-repair-deficient labels remain powerful immunotherapy biomarkers, but they do not describe a single biological state. Tumors can share the same umbrella label while differing in immune infiltration, suppressive context, and therapeutic sensitivity.

## Key Points

- Hypermutation and mismatch-repair deficiency increase the chance of immunogenicity, but they do not guarantee a uniformly inflamed tumor microenvironment.
- Gastric MSI-high cancers can split into subgroups with distinct immune-suppression features and different survival outcomes.
- In endometrial cancer, `MLH1` hypermethylated tumors can look colder and do worse than Lynch-like `MLH1`-mutated tumors despite both living under the dMMR umbrella.
- These observations matter clinically because they warn against flattening all MSI-H or dMMR tumors into a single checkpoint-sensitive class.
- The concept sits between biomarker optimism and resistance biology: a tumor can be biomarker-positive yet still be too cold, too suppressed, or too poorly organized to derive equal benefit.

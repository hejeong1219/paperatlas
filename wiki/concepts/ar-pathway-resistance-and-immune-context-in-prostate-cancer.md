# AR Pathway Resistance and Immune Context in Prostate Cancer

Advanced prostate cancer remains organized around androgen-receptor biology, but resistance is genetically diverse and clinically layered. A useful framework has to hold both truths at once: AR remains central, yet progression increasingly involves co-alterations, pathway diversification, and later immune or lineage-state consequences.

## Key Points

- Metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer contains frequent `AR`, `TP53`, `PTEN`, and DNA-repair alterations, with high rates of clinically actionable lesions.
- On-target AR resistance can emerge through ligand-binding-domain changes that convert antagonists into agonists.
- Rational drug design can sometimes overcome such on-target mutations without abandoning the pathway.
- Precision oncology in advanced prostate cancer depends on sequencing because AR dominance does not mean genomic simplicity.
- This axis sets the stage for later splice-variant, DNA-repair, and immune-context papers in the prostate corpus.

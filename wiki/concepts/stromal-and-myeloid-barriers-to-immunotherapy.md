# Stromal and Myeloid Barriers to Immunotherapy

Some resistant tumors are not defined primarily by antigen loss or interferon failure, but by a stromal and myeloid ecosystem that blocks lymphocyte recruitment, positioning, activation, or persistence.

## Key Points

- Systemic inflammatory signals such as `IL-6` and `CRP` can mark a host state associated with poor checkpoint outcomes.
- Fibroblast heterogeneity matters: some CAF states help construct immunosuppressive or poorly penetrable tumor architecture, while others may directly modulate antigen presentation and CD4 biology.
- Myeloid programs such as CSF1R-driven macrophage states can actively suppress productive T-cell immunity yet create combinatorial vulnerabilities when reprogrammed.
- Vascular state also matters: immune benefit from antiangiogenic therapy may depend on whether treatment generates permissive structures such as HEVs rather than merely pruning vessels.
- This concept complements TGF-beta exclusion by widening the focus from fibroblast signaling alone to a broader stromal-macrophage-vascular system.

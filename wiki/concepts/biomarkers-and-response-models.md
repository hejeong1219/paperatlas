# Biomarkers and Response Models

This axis focuses on what the field uses to explain and predict response, including antigen quality, T-cell behavior, B-cell states, TLS maturity, and spatial context.

## Key Questions

- Which biomarkers best connect upstream antigen design with downstream clinical response?
- Are TLS and B-cell features predictive enough to guide therapeutic strategy?
- What combinations of biomarkers are likely to be most useful?

## Key Points

- Response prediction increasingly requires combining tumor-intrinsic and immune-state variables rather than choosing one biomarker winner.
- `TMB` and T-cell-inflamed transcriptomic programs capture different dimensions of response biology and remain useful precisely because they are not redundant.
- TLS-derived scores may operationalize local immune organization in ways that complement PD-L1 and broader inflamed-signature models.
- Biomarker-negative or biomarker-resistant states can still be mechanistically informative when they point toward bypass strategies such as CTLA-4-containing regimens or microbiota-directed interventions.
- In oncogene-driven lung cancer, biomarkers such as PD-L1 or TMB do not transport cleanly from smoking-associated NSCLC.

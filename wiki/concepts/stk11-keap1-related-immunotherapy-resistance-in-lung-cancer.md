# STK11-KEAP1-Related Immunotherapy Resistance in Lung Cancer

This concept page tracks one of the most reproducible genotype-linked checkpoint resistance programs in lung cancer: tumors with `STK11/LKB1` and related `KEAP1` alterations often behave as immunologically unresponsive disease, yet later work suggests that this state may be partially bypassed with different immune combinations.

## Key Points

- `STK11/LKB1` loss in KRAS-mutant lung adenocarcinoma is strongly associated with poor benefit from PD-1 axis blockade.
- The biology is usually expressed as an immune-cold or poorly inflamed microenvironment, not merely as high-risk genotype in the abstract.
- `KEAP1` alterations often travel with similarly poor checkpoint outcomes and reinforce the idea of a resistant lung-cancer immune state.
- Later studies suggest that `CTLA-4` containing combinations can recover some benefit in these genotypes, reframing them as selective-combination biomarkers rather than absolute contraindications.
- This axis links tumor genetics, microenvironmental phenotype, and regimen choice more tightly than many earlier biomarker models.

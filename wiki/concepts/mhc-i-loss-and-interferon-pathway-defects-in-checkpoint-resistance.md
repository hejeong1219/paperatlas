# MHC-I Loss and Interferon Pathway Defects in Checkpoint Resistance

One of the most reusable resistance frameworks in immuno-oncology is that tumors can survive active immune pressure either by losing antigen display through MHC-I/HLA failure or by becoming unable to respond to interferon cues that normally amplify immune killing.

## Key Points

- `B2M`, HLA, and related antigen-presentation lesions are among the clearest mechanisms of acquired immune escape after checkpoint blockade or adoptive T-cell therapy.
- `JAK1/2` loss-of-function mutations create a complementary route by disabling interferon-gamma sensing, which blunts antiproliferative signaling and adaptive immune gene induction.
- These defects can appear as primary resistance, acquired resistance, or broader evolutionary immunoediting during progression and metastasis.
- Not all lesions are irreversible structural deletions: epigenetic silencing programs such as the `USP22-EZH2` axis may phenocopy antigen-presentation loss while remaining pharmacologically targetable.
- The framework generalizes across melanoma, lung cancer, colorectal cancer, and neoantigen-directed cell therapy, even though the exact genomic lesions differ by setting.

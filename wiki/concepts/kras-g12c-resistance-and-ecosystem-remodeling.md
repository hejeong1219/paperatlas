# KRAS G12C Resistance and Ecosystem Remodeling

KRAS G12C inhibitor resistance begins as adaptive heterogeneity and can mature into whole-ecosystem remodeling. The resistant state is often not explained by one clean secondary mutation, but by a mixture of signaling bypass, clonal reshaping, quiescence escape, and tumor-microenvironment rewiring.

## Key Points

- Early response to KRAS G12C inhibition is non-uniform, with some cells entering quiescence and others rapidly reactivating proliferative signaling.
- Clinical resistance can emerge through KRAS-independent MAPK restoration, clonal selection, and strong non-cell-autonomous changes in angiogenesis, coagulation, complement, and immune evasion.
- Rapid-autopsy and paired-sample studies suggest that distinct metastatic sites can evolve different resistance logics within the same patient.
- This resistance axis is therefore both tumor-intrinsic and ecosystem-level.
- It is a useful counterexample to models that expect every targeted therapy to fail through one recurrent gatekeeper lesion.

# Bispecific Antibodies in Oncology

Bispecific antibodies are no longer a niche engineering format; they now function as a major therapeutic platform whose success depends on target geometry, immune-cell recruitment, toxicity control, and the durability of target dependence.

## Key Points

- The field now spans classic CD3-engagers, dual-tumor-target constructs, and broader multispecific designs intended to improve selectivity or block parallel signaling routes.
- Hematologic successes established the platform, but recent solid-tumor trials such as DLL3-directed tarlatamab in SCLC have moved bispecifics into randomized survival-positive territory.
- HER2-directed agents such as zanidatamab show that bispecific logic is not limited to T-cell engagers; dual-epitope tumor targeting can itself deepen signaling blockade and receptor clustering effects.
- Resistance pressure can act through antigen loss, heterogeneous target expression, lineage change, insufficient immune-cell recruitment, or toxicity-limited dosing.
- Good bispecific design is therefore not only about stronger binding, but about choosing targets whose biology remains stable under treatment pressure.

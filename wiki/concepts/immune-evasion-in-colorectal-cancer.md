# Immune Evasion in Colorectal Cancer

Colorectal cancer uses at least two distinct immune-evasion logics: immunogenic MSI-high tumors can evolve antigen-presentation loss, whereas more common immune-poor tumors are often dominated by exclusion programs such as WNT-driven T-cell absence.

## Key Points

- MSI-high colorectal cancers can remain highly mutated and lymphocyte-rich while still acquiring hard genetic lesions in B2M, HLA, and related antigen-presentation pathways.
- These lesions are evidence of immunoediting, not immune ignorance: the tumor was seen by the immune system and then selected for escape.
- Across colorectal cancer more broadly, WNT/beta-catenin activation correlates with reduced T-cell infiltration and a colder immune state.
- This distinction matters clinically because checkpoint failure in MSI-high disease often reflects acquired escape, whereas failure in MSS disease often reflects baseline exclusion.
- The framework generalizes beyond CRC and helps interpret immune-cold versus immune-edited phenotypes across other tumors.

# CAR-T Cell Resistance and Design Strategies

CAR-T resistance is not a single relapse mechanism. It sits at the intersection of product quality, tumor adaptation, antigen architecture, trafficking, persistence, and the suppressive microenvironment.

## Key Points

- CAR-T failure can arise from dysfunctional or exhausted infused cells, tumor-intrinsic escape, or host and microenvironmental barriers.
- Antigen loss is only one part of the problem; poor persistence, tonic signaling, lack of accessory ligands, apoptosis resistance, and suppressive myeloid or stromal niches also matter.
- Engineering responses include dual or tandem targeting, armoured CARs, TRUCKs, regional delivery, safety switches, logic-gated receptors, and manufacturing optimization.
- AML highlights one of the hardest unresolved CAR-T problems because many candidate targets overlap with normal hematopoietic compartments.
- This framework helps connect hematologic CAR-T relapse literature with solid-tumor engineering strategies instead of treating them as unrelated subfields.

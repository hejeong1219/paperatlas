## Why This Topic Exists

- It gathers both foundational immune-framework papers and concrete resistance mechanisms in one place.
- It lets the wiki compare tumor-intrinsic escape, cell-therapy design failure, and trial-level efficacy without flattening them into a single explanation.
- It now also connects antibody-drug conjugate resistance, EGFR TKI escape, and immune-editing in colorectal cancer, so the topic no longer treats immunotherapy resistance as a checkpoint-only problem.
- The batch 3 additions make the topic more mechanistically complete by linking interferon failure, MHC-I or HLA loss, stromal exclusion, and heterogeneity inside MSI-H or dMMR biomarker groups.
- The batch 4 additions extend the topic into CAR-T platform resistance, macrophage or fibroblast or vascular barriers, systemic inflammatory correlates, and TLS-linked response organization.
- The batch 5 additions bring in host-level microbiota context, soluble and metabolic suppressors, genotype-linked lung cancer resistance states, and cross-tumor biomarker models that explain why checkpoint benefit still fragments inside apparently similar clinical groups.
- The batch 6 additions extend the topic into EGFR- and KRAS-driven targeted-therapy resistance states that directly intersect with immune failure through EMT, histologic transformation, MET-STING suppression, and ecosystem remodeling under KRAS G12C inhibition.
- The batch 7 additions widen the map into endocrine and androgen-axis resistance, showing that molecular escape in breast and prostate cancer can also reshape immune context, while EGFR-mutant NSCLC salvage regimens continue to define the boundary conditions for when checkpoint therapy can still contribute.
- It now also has a manuscript-side anchor so future papers can be ranked against the active resistance review rather than only against abstract topic labels.

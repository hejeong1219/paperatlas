# Tertiary lymphoid structures critical for prognosis in endometrial cancer patients

Human endometrial cancer study showing that TLS and B-cell organization carry strong prognostic value and can be operationalized through a practical biomarker strategy.

## Key Points

- Studies B-cell states and TLS architecture in endometrial cancer using single-cell RNA sequencing and tissue-based validation.
- Identifies naive B cells, cycling or germinal-center-like B cells, and antibody-secreting cells within the TLS-associated immune ecosystem.
- Finds TLS in a minority of molecularly classified endometrial cancers, with enrichment in mismatch-repair-deficient and POLE-mutant disease.
- Reports strong favorable prognostic impact of TLS independent of clinicopathological and molecular factors.
- Proposes `L1CAM` as a practical TLS-associated biomarker that could make TLS assessment easier in clinical cohorts.

## Relevance

- Directly relevant to the B-cell neoantigen proposal because it reinforces the B-cell and TLS side of the project in a human tumor type where hypermutation and antigenicity are already clinically meaningful.
- Useful for comparing how organized B-cell niches behave across ovarian, head and neck, gastric, nasopharyngeal, and endometrial cancers.
- Helps connect molecular subtype, antigenicity, and mature lymphoid structure into one clinically interpretable framework.

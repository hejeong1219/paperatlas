# The dynamic evolution of lineage switch under CD19 CAR-T treatment in non-KMT2A rearranged B-ALL patients

This paper argues that lineage switch after CD19 CAR-T is not confined to the classic KMT2A-rearranged setting and can arise through plasticity programs in non-KMT2A-r B-ALL.

## Key Points

- Describes two non-KMT2A-r B-ALL cases that relapsed as AML after CD19 CAR-T, expanding the known lineage-switch phenotype.
- Uses multi-omic sampling to show persistence of founding lesions with phenotypic conversion, supporting reprogramming rather than a completely unrelated second leukemia.
- Highlights recurrent truncating changes in `BCOR` or `BCORL1` as potentially important in enabling myeloid differentiation under immune pressure.
- Shows persistent driver architecture such as `FLT3-ITD` or `EP300::ZNF384` across the switch, alongside emergence or expansion of BCOR-family lesions.
- Proposes a third lineage-switch paradigm in which B-progenitor-like blasts with latent myeloid features transdifferentiate under therapeutic pressure.

## Open Questions

- The paper is based on only two cases, so `BCOR/BCORL1` should be treated as a plausible mechanism rather than a settled universal driver.
- It remains unclear whether these lesions independently cause lineage switch or mainly mark a permissive plasticity state.

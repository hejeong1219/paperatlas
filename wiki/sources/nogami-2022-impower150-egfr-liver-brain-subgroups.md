# IMpower150 Final Exploratory Analyses for Atezolizumab Plus Bevacizumab and Chemotherapy in Key NSCLC Patient Subgroups With EGFR Mutations or Metastases in the Liver or Brain

This analysis remains important because it is one of the clearest clinical signals that a bevacizumab-containing immunochemotherapy regimen might do more in EGFR-mutant NSCLC than checkpoint therapy alone.

## Key Points

- Nogami et al. report final exploratory subgroup analyses from IMpower150, comparing `ABCP` against `BCP` and `ACP` in metastatic nonsquamous NSCLC.
- In the sensitizing `EGFR` subgroup, including patients previously treated with TKIs, `ABCP` showed a survival signal over `BCP`, whereas `ACP` did not.
- The paper matters because it suggests bevacizumab may be functionally important in making immunochemotherapy usable in selected EGFR-mutant disease.
- Similar exploratory benefit patterns were seen in patients with liver metastases, and there was a suggestion that ABCP might delay new brain metastases.
- In the wiki, this is a practical counterpoint to negative checkpoint-only studies in EGFR-mutant NSCLC.

# Pan-tumor genomic biomarkers for PD-1 checkpoint blockade-based immunotherapy

This study is one of the clearest field-defining attempts to reduce checkpoint response into two partially independent axes: neoantigenicity, approximated by `TMB`, and pre-existing immune activation, approximated by a T-cell-inflamed gene-expression profile.

## Key Points

- Cristescu et al. analyzed more than 300 samples across 22 tumor types from multiple KEYNOTE studies and found that `TMB` and a T-cell-inflamed `GEP` each predicted pembrolizumab response.
- The most durable contribution of the paper is the claim that these biomarkers are only weakly correlated, implying that mutation load and immune activation capture different biological dimensions.
- Joint stratification generated a practical resistance framework: some tumors are low in both axes, whereas others are mutation-rich but poorly inflamed or inflamed despite lower mutation burden.
- This matters for the wiki because it provides a reusable language for organizing biomarker-positive but treatment-resistant states without collapsing everything into one variable.
- The paper also helps explain why later work on TLS, interferon signaling, microbiota, and stromal exclusion can all coexist with TMB rather than simply replacing it.

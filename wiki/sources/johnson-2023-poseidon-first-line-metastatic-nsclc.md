# Durvalumab With or Without Tremelimumab in Combination With Chemotherapy as First-Line Therapy for Metastatic Non-Small-Cell Lung Cancer: The Phase III POSEIDON Study

POSEIDON is important in this corpus because it operationalizes a resistance-bypass hypothesis at phase 3 scale: if PD-L1 axis blockade alone leaves too many patients behind, a brief CTLA-4-containing priming strategy may broaden benefit in metastatic NSCLC.

## Key Points

- The trial compared chemotherapy alone against chemotherapy plus durvalumab, and against chemotherapy plus durvalumab with a limited-course tremelimumab component in metastatic EGFR/ALK wild-type NSCLC.
- Durvalumab plus chemotherapy improved progression-free survival, but the three-drug arm was the one that also showed an overall survival advantage over chemotherapy alone.
- The paper matters mechanistically because it suggests that a subset of metastatic NSCLC requires more than PD-L1 blockade layered onto chemotherapy; a broader priming strategy may be needed to overcome baseline immune insufficiency.
- Toxicity increased with the tremelimumab-containing regimen, so the signal is clinically meaningful only if the added immune activation is judged worth the added cost and adverse events.
- In this wiki, POSEIDON functions as a clinical anchor beside genotype-focused rescue papers such as the 2024 `STK11/KEAP1` study, reminding us that some resistance-bypass logic is already being tested in frontline practice.

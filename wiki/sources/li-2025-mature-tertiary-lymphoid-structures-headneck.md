# Mature tertiary lymphoid structures evoke intra-tumoral T and B cell responses via progenitor exhausted CD4+ T cells in head and neck cancer

Human HNSCC study showing that mature TLS organize coordinated B-cell and T-cell programs and are associated with better survival and improved checkpoint response.

## Key Points

- Uses single-cell RNA sequencing, antigen receptor sequencing, and spatial transcriptomics on human head and neck squamous cell carcinoma samples with different TLS states.
- Distinguishes mature TLS from non-mature TLS and shows that mature TLS are enriched for stem-like T cells and B cells across multiple maturation stages.
- Highlights progenitor exhausted CD4-positive T cells with Tfh-like features as a coordinating population that supports both plasma-cell generation and CD8-positive T-cell activation.
- Links mature TLS status to improved overall survival and better immune checkpoint blockade response in HNSCC.
- Provides a mechanistic human cancer explanation for why TLS maturity, not just TLS presence, may matter for local antitumor immunity.

## Relevance

- Strong direct fit for the B-cell neoantigen proposal because it sharpens the immune-context side of the project around mature TLS, B-cell maturation, and local helper circuitry.
- Useful for interpreting how future neoantigen candidates might matter differently in tumors with organized versus poorly organized lymphoid niches.
- Extends the existing TLS corpus with a recent mechanistic human cancer paper in a high-impact venue.

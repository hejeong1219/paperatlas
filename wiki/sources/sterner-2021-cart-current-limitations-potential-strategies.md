# CAR-T cell therapy: current limitations and potential strategies

Sterner and Sterner provide a practical engineering-oriented review of why CAR-T succeeds in some settings and stalls in others, especially when moving from blood cancers into solid tumors.

## Key Points

- The review centers the main CAR-T obstacles as antigen escape, on-target off-tumor effects, poor trafficking, limited tumor infiltration, immunosuppressive microenvironment, and treatment-related toxicities.
- It emphasizes that many of these barriers are interconnected rather than independent, which is why simple single-variable solutions often disappoint.
- The paper is especially useful for its strategy framing: dual or tandem targeting, regional delivery, microenvironment modulation, and iterative receptor engineering are treated as matched responses to distinct failure modes.
- Compared with later reviews, Sterner is less exhaustive mechanistically but more straightforward as a design-and-limitation overview.
- It remains a good entry point for understanding why hematologic success did not immediately generalize to solid tumors.

# Zanidatamab for HER2-amplified, unresectable, locally advanced or metastatic biliary tract cancer (HERIZON-BTC-01): a multicentre, single-arm, phase 2b study

This initial HERIZON-BTC-01 report established zanidatamab as one of the clearest HER2-directed signals in biliary tract cancer, showing that dual-epitope HER2 engagement can generate durable activity in a molecularly selected, treatment-refractory disease.

## Key Points

- Enrolled 87 patients overall, with the primary efficacy cohort centered on 80 patients with HER2-amplified and HER2-overexpressing disease.
- Confirmed objective responses by independent central review were seen in 33 of 80 patients, for a response rate of 41.3%.
- Median follow-up at the initial report was 12.4 months, with 21% of patients still on treatment at the data cutoff.
- Grade 3 treatment-related adverse events occurred in 18% of patients; diarrhea and decreased ejection fraction were the most frequent serious toxicities, and there were no treatment-related deaths.
- Conceptually, the paper shows that HER2 remains a tractable precision-immunotherapy target outside breast and gastric cancer, but it also sets up an obvious resistance question: how stable is HER2 dependence under prolonged bispecific-antibody pressure?

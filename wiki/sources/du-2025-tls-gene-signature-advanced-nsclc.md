# Tertiary lymphoid structures gene signature predicts response to immunotherapy plus chemotherapy in advanced non-small cell lung cancer

This study pushes TLS biology from descriptive pathology toward a clinically usable composite biomarker in advanced NSCLC, arguing that a TLS-derived transcriptomic score can add predictive value beyond PD-L1 alone for first-line chemoimmunotherapy.

## Key Points

- Du et al. screened 17 TLS-related signatures across randomized-trial datasets and selected `TLS signature 3` as the most informative predictor of first-line chemoimmunotherapy benefit in advanced NSCLC.
- In the ORIENT-11 cohort, TLS signature 3 high tumors showed longer progression-free and overall survival than TLS-low tumors, and the association persisted in multivariable models.
- The paper is especially useful because it does not frame TLS only as a binary pathologic feature; it treats TLS-associated immune organization as a measurable transcriptomic state that can travel across datasets.
- Signature 3 correlated with PD-L1 and broader immune-inflamed phenotypes, but the authors argue it still adds information in PD-L1-negative disease, which is the clinically interesting claim.
- In this wiki, the paper strengthens the idea that organized local immunity is not just prognostic after surgery or in selected sarcoma cohorts, but may also help explain why some metastatic NSCLC tumors convert chemoimmunotherapy into durable benefit.

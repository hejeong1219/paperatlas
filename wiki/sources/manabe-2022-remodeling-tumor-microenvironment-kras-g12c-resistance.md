# Remodeling of the tumor/tumor microenvironment ecosystem during KRAS G12C inhibitor clinical resistance in lung cancer

This rapid-autopsy study shows that clinical KRAS G12C resistance is not just a story of pathway reactivation inside tumor cells; it can involve whole-ecosystem remodeling, including angiogenesis, coagulation, complement activation, and immunologic evasion.

## Key Points

- Manabe and Bivona analyze a KRAS G12C-mutant lung adenocarcinoma before treatment and after clinical resistance to direct KRAS G12C inhibition.
- Resistant tumors showed reduced KRAS G12C mutant allele fraction together with restored MAPK signaling despite no clear new downstream KRAS-pathway mutation.
- The study highlights non-cell-autonomous shifts including complement, coagulation, angiogenic programs, and evidence of immune escape, broadening the resistance frame beyond on-target mutation hunting.
- This is valuable in the wiki because it connects targeted-therapy progression to tumor-microenvironment rewiring, making it relevant to immunotherapy resistance rather than only KRAS pharmacology.
- The paper pairs naturally with earlier adaptive-state studies and later rapid-autopsy resistance reports by showing that the resistant endpoint can be ecologically complex even when a single target initiated the response.

# Proteogenomic characterization of human colon and rectal cancer

Foundational colorectal proteogenomics paper showing that proteome data can prioritize drivers and subtype structure that are only partly visible through genomics or transcriptomics alone.

## Key Points

- Integrates proteomic analysis with previously characterized TCGA colon and rectal tumors.
- Shows that mRNA abundance does not reliably predict protein abundance differences between tumors.
- Identifies five proteomic subtypes, with clinically and biologically meaningful structure that refines transcriptomic classification.
- Demonstrates that many copy-number cis and trans effects seen at the RNA level do not extend cleanly to the protein level.
- Uses the proteome to prioritize candidate driver genes, including candidates within chromosome 20q alterations.

## Relevance

- Highly relevant to the ptmanchor manuscript as an early landmark for why protein-level interpretation changes biological inference relative to RNA-focused analysis.
- Supports the core methodological intuition that abundance-aware protein context is necessary before drawing strong conclusions about downstream signaling regulation.
- Valuable foundational background for PTM-aware analysis across clinical tumor cohorts.

# Neutralizing GDF-15 can overcome anti-PD-1 and anti-PD-L1 resistance in solid tumours

This paper elevates `GDF-15` from a biomarker-like stress cytokine to an actionable resistance mediator, arguing that neutralization can reopen T-cell access and restore checkpoint activity in tumors already deemed PD-1 or PD-L1 refractory.

## Key Points

- Melero et al. combine preclinical rationale with a first-in-human `visugromab + nivolumab` study in patients whose cancers had already progressed on prior anti-PD-1 or anti-PD-L1 therapy.
- The mechanistic claim is that tumor-derived `GDF-15` limits antitumor immunity partly by interfering with immune-cell trafficking, especially the LFA-1-ICAM-1 axis that supports T-cell entry into tumors.
- On-treatment biopsies showed increased T-cell infiltration, proliferation, interferon-gamma-related signaling, and granzyme-B-positive cytotoxic activity, supporting a real pharmacodynamic immune effect rather than simple late nivolumab activity.
- Clinical responses were not pan-tumor uniform; the paper highlights non-squamous NSCLC and urothelial cancer as contexts where deep or durable responses were seen despite prior checkpoint refractoriness.
- In the wiki's resistance map, this is an important bridge paper because it shows a soluble suppressive factor can be targeted to convert checkpoint-resistant disease back toward an immune-inflamed state.

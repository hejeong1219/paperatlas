# ESR1 ligand-binding domain mutations in hormone-resistant breast cancer

This is one of the foundational papers that made `ESR1` mutations central to endocrine resistance in metastatic ER-positive breast cancer.

## Key Points

- Toy et al. identified recurrent `ESR1` ligand-binding-domain mutations in metastatic ER-positive breast cancers exposed to estrogen deprivation or anti-estrogen therapy.
- Recurrent alterations such as `Y537S`, `Y537N`, and `D538G` favored an agonist receptor conformation and enabled estrogen-independent ER signaling.
- The study matters because it converted endocrine resistance from a vague clinical phenotype into a recurrent, mechanistically coherent molecular lesion.
- It also immediately implied a therapeutic direction: more potent ER antagonism or strategies not defeated by ligand-independent receptor activity.
- In this wiki, this paper functions as the breast-cancer analogue of other acquired-resistance landmarks that identify a new dominant escape axis.

# Bispecific antibodies for B-cell lymphoma: promises, unknowns, and opportunities

_Blood 141(5):467, 2023 — review._ PMID: [36322929](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36322929/) · DOI: [10.1182/blood.2021011994](https://doi.org/10.1182/blood.2021011994)

## Summary

Comprehensive Memorial Sloan Kettering review covering CD20×CD3 and other bispecific antibody (BsAb) classes for B-cell non-Hodgkin lymphoma (B-NHL). Anti-CD20×CD3 BsAb (mosunetuzumab, glofitamab, epcoritamab, odronextamab) are the headline class — single-agent activity in heavily pretreated patients with manageable T-cell overactivation toxicity, positioning them as the next post-CAR-T therapeutic milestone in B-NHL. The review walks through structural classes (Fc-bearing IgG-like vs no-Fc BiTE-like), key engineering choices (knobs-into-holes for heavy-chain pairing, CD20 affinity tuning, target epitope geometry), the determinants of tumor cell killing in vitro (target density, affinity, synapse formation, costimulation balance), and the open clinical-development questions: optimal line of therapy, combinations (with chemo, with anti-CD79b ADC, with CAR-T sequencing), CRS/ICANS mitigation, and pharmacodynamic biomarkers of response/resistance. The framing matters because BsAb are off-the-shelf (vs CAR-T's logistical burden) and because mechanism overlaps with CAR-T (T-cell redirection without HLA restriction) — making the resistance landscape between the two classes deeply related.

## Key Points

- **Class-defining drug**: anti-CD20×CD3 BsAb redirect endogenous T cells to CD20⁺ B-NHL cells — MHC-independent killing, off-the-shelf product (vs autologous CAR-T).
- **Approved / near-approved CD20×CD3 BsAb**: mosunetuzumab (FL), glofitamab (DLBCL), epcoritamab (DLBCL), odronextamab — single-agent ORR 50–80% in heavily pretreated B-NHL depending on disease and dose.
- **Format diversity matters**: IgG-like (full Fc, longer half-life, FcγR-mediated effects retained) vs no-Fc BiTE-like (smaller, no Fc, shorter half-life, no FcγR effects); engineering choice changes pharmacology and toxicity.
- **Target choice within B-cell antigens**: BsAb cotargeting CD3 + CD20 outperformed CD3 + CD22/CD37/CD70/CD79b/CD138/HLA-DR for cytotoxicity even at matched antigen expression — suggesting target-intrinsic factors (epitope position, synapse geometry) beyond expression level.
- **In vitro determinants of killing**: target affinity, antigen density, BsAb size/flexibility, epitope position on cell surface, ease of immune synapse formation, costimulatory/coinhibitory balance.
- **Toxicity dominated by T-cell overactivation**: CRS (cytokine release syndrome), ICANS (neurotoxicity); step-up dosing and tocilizumab-based mitigation now standard. Distinct from CAR-T but mechanistically related.
- **Resistance angle (relevant for the user's manuscripts)**: review highlights antigen escape (CD20 loss, like CD19 loss in CAR-T), T-cell exhaustion in heavily pretreated patients, and tumor microenvironment factors as the main resistance modes — directly mirroring the CAR-T resistance landscape.
- **Combination logic discussed**: with anti-CD79b ADC (polatuzumab), with chemo (R-CHOP-like backbones), with anti-PD-1, and pre/post-CAR-T sequencing — these combinations are now in active phase 2/3 trials.
- **Open biomarker question**: there is no validated PD biomarker of BsAb response/resistance — the review explicitly calls this the most pressing gap.

## Methods

Narrative review; covers preclinical structural biology, in vitro cytotoxicity determinants, then traverses clinical development of major CD20×CD3 BsAb plus next-generation targets (CD19, ROR1, BCMA cross-reads). No primary data; synthesizes pivotal trial data and mechanistic studies.

## Limitations

- Review (2023 cutoff): newer combinations and post-2023 phase 3 readouts not covered.
- Heavy emphasis on CD20×CD3; B-NHL targets beyond CD20 (CD19, CD22, CD79b for redirection) are discussed but not in depth.
- Limited coverage of solid-tumor BsAb beyond noting general field — focus is B-NHL.
- Pharmacodynamic biomarker section is largely "open question" rather than recommendation, reflecting field state at writing.

## Open Questions

- What is the durable PD biomarker for BsAb response — circulating T-cell phenotype, tumor T-cell infiltration, CD20 density, or something else?
- Optimal sequencing of BsAb and CAR-T: which first, and does pre-exposure to one limit the other?
- Can anti-CD20×CD3 be combined with checkpoint blockade safely, and does that overcome T-cell exhaustion in heavily pretreated patients?
- Is CD20-low / CD20-loss escape a clonal selection event (like CD19 loss) or a transient adaptation? Genomic studies in BsAb-failure patients are needed.
- For patients who fail both CAR-T and BsAb (CD19/CD20 double-loss), what salvage targets remain — CD22, CD79b, BCMA?

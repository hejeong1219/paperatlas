# Proteogenomic characterization of 2002 human cancers reveals pan-cancer molecular subtypes and associated pathways

Large human pan-cancer proteogenomics compendium showing that protein-level subtypes and pathway activity can cut across tumor lineages and reveal genome-proteome relationships not fully captured by transcriptomics alone.

## Key Points

- Assembles proteomics and matched multi-omics data from 2002 primary human tumors across 14 cancer types and 17 studies.
- Defines 11 proteome-based pan-cancer subtypes that span tissue lineages and expose common pathway structure across tumors.
- Shows that protein abundance broadly correlates with mRNA and copy number, but also contains important discordant signals with biological meaning.
- Uses the large compendium to study how somatic pathway alterations relate to downstream pathway activity inferred from proteome and transcriptome layers.
- Extends earlier CPTAC-style work into a larger cross-study proteogenomic background resource for pan-cancer interpretation.

## Relevance

- Strongly relevant to the ptmanchor manuscript as a foundational pan-cancer reference for why protein-level modeling deserves its own analytical treatment rather than being reduced to transcript-level inference.
- Useful background for abundance-aware PTM interpretation because it frames the proteome as an informative system layer with pathway structure and subtype resolution.
- Not a direct phosphosite-correction paper, but an important landmark context paper for any pan-cancer protein-plus-PTM methodology.

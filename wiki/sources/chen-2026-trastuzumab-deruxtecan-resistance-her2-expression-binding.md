# Trastuzumab Deruxtecan Resistance via Loss of HER2 Expression and Binding

This is the clearest mechanistic paper in the current batch for ADC escape: it shows that trastuzumab deruxtecan resistance can arise not only from generalized drug resistance but from direct loss of target abundance or target engagement itself.

## Key Points

- In paired patient samples before and after trastuzumab deruxtecan, 49% showed major HER2 decreases at progression, and 52% of those had complete HER2 loss.
- Experimental systems linked HER2 downregulation to reduced trastuzumab deruxtecan internalization and marked increases in the drug concentration required for growth inhibition.
- Identified HER2 extracellular-domain mutations in the trastuzumab binding interface, including V597M and P593R, as direct resistance mechanisms that impair binding.
- Resistant cells remained vulnerable to the shared deruxtecan payload when it was delivered through another target, supporting low-dose multi-target ADC combinations such as HER2 plus TROP2.
- The paper shifts the resistance model for ADCs toward a target-mediated framework: if payload sensitivity persists but delivery fails, then antigen density, epitope integrity, and internalization kinetics become the key variables.

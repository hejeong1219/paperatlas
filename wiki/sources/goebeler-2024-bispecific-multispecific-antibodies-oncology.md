# Bispecific and multispecific antibodies in oncology: opportunities and challenges

This review is a useful bridge paper between mechanism and clinic because it explains why bispecific antibodies have expanded so quickly, what engineering choices matter most, and where resistance or toxicity are likely to shape the next wave.

## Key Points

- Summarizes a field with more than 200 bispecific candidates in preclinical or clinical development, most centered on immune-cell engagement.
- Argues that target selection is the central bottleneck, especially in solid tumors where tumor selectivity and normal-tissue sparing are hard to separate.
- Emphasizes that bispecifics can sometimes overcome suppressive microenvironmental signals by physically redirecting immune cells and adding stimulatory cues at the tumor site.
- Highlights how multispecific or dual-tumor-target constructs might reduce resistance relative to single-target therapy by broadening target engagement or blocking parallel pathways.
- Reviews cytokine release syndrome mitigation strategies, including steroid pretreatment, step-up dosing, subcutaneous delivery, altered CD3 affinity, and prodrug-style designs.

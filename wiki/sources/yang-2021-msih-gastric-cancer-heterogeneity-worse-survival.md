# Heterogeneity of MSI-H gastric cancer identifies a subtype with worse survival

This study makes a simple but durable point: even within MSI-high gastric cancer, there are biologically distinct subgroups, and one of them carries a more suppressive immune program and worse prognosis.

## Key Points

- Yang et al. used consensus clustering on MSI-high stomach adenocarcinoma cases from TCGA and an Asian validation cohort to define two MSI-high subgroups, `MSI-H1` and `MSI-H2`.
- The worse-outcome subgroup (`MSI-H1`) was enriched for immune-suppression-associated features despite sharing the broader MSI-high label.
- The paper directly challenges the shorthand assumption that MSI-high automatically means favorable prognosis or uniformly strong checkpoint sensitivity.
- Its main contribution is stratificational rather than mechanistic: it pushes MSI-high from a binary category toward a heterogeneous landscape that needs finer immune annotation.
- This logic lines up well with later endometrial data showing that even dMMR tumors can differ markedly depending on the route and context of mismatch-repair loss.

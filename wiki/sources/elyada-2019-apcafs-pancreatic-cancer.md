# Cross-Species Single-Cell Analysis of Pancreatic Ductal Adenocarcinoma Reveals Antigen-Presenting Cancer-Associated Fibroblasts

Elyada et al. is a landmark fibroblast heterogeneity paper because it introduced `apCAFs` and made CAF biology directly relevant to antigen presentation and immune organization, not only matrix production.

## Key Points

- The study used single-cell RNA sequencing across human and mouse PDAC to refine stromal heterogeneity.
- It confirmed previously described `myCAF` and `iCAF` states and added a new `apCAF` population expressing `MHC class II` genes and `CD74`.
- These antigen-presenting CAFs lacked classical co-stimulatory molecules, which is part of why the paper raised as many mechanistic questions as it answered.
- In model systems, apCAFs were able to activate CD4 T cells in an antigen-dependent manner, supporting the claim that CAFs can shape adaptive immunity more directly than older stromal models suggested.
- The paper also emphasizes that CAF states are dynamic and interconvertible rather than fixed lineages.

# Senescence as a therapeutically relevant response to CDK4/6 inhibitors

This review is a useful conceptual frame for CDK4/6 therapy because it distinguishes senescence from quiescence and asks when CDK4/6-induced growth arrest becomes biologically exploitable rather than merely cytostatic.

## Key Points

- Wagner and Gil review how CDK4/6 inhibitors can induce either quiescence or senescence depending on cellular context.
- The key translational point is that senescence is not just durable arrest; it also carries secretory, metabolic, and immune consequences that may shape treatment durability.
- The paper highlights the possibility of sequencing `CDK4/6` inhibitors with `senolytics` or other interventions to exploit senescent states rather than letting them persist ambiguously.
- In this wiki, the review provides the conceptual scaffold for more mechanistic papers comparing the immune quality of different senescence programs.
- It also helps connect breast-cancer endocrine therapy literature to broader treatment-induced state transitions across cancer.

# Rapid non-uniform adaptation to conformation-specific KRAS(G12C) inhibition

This paper established a key principle for KRAS G12C therapy: even before stable genetic resistance emerges, treated cancer cells can diverge into distinct adaptive fates, with some entering quiescence and others rapidly finding routes back to proliferation.

## Key Points

- Xue et al. used single-cell-level analyses to show that KRAS G12C inhibition produces a non-uniform early response rather than a synchronized collapse of the tumor population.
- Some cells become trapped in a low-KRAS-output quiescent state, whereas others restore signaling and resume growth through upstream support such as `EGFR` or `AURKA`.
- The study is mechanistically important because KRAS G12C inhibitors bind the inactive GDP-bound state, so newly synthesized KRAS and upstream context become central determinants of escape.
- In the wiki's resistance logic, this paper marks the difference between rapid adaptive persistence and later, more fixed clinical resistance.
- It also provides the conceptual foundation for later KRAS G12C ecosystem papers showing that resistance is both heterogeneous and strongly shaped by cell state.

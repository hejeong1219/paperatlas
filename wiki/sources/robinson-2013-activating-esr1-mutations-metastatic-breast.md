# Activating ESR1 mutations in hormone-resistant metastatic breast cancer

Published alongside Toy et al., this paper independently established `ESR1` mutation as a recurrent acquired-resistance mechanism in metastatic ER-positive breast cancer and broadened the mutational spectrum.

## Key Points

- Robinson et al. identified multiple activating `ESR1` ligand-binding-domain mutations in metastatic ER-positive breast cancers after endocrine therapy exposure.
- The work expanded the mutation catalogue beyond the most recurrent hotspots and showed that these variants retained constitutive activity while remaining variably sensitive to anti-estrogen agents in vitro.
- Its importance lies partly in convergence: two independent groups arrived at the same biological conclusion at the same moment, which accelerated field acceptance.
- The paper also highlighted that similar `ESR1` mutations could appear in endometrial cancer, hinting at a broader estrogen-receptor biology beyond breast cancer.
- In this wiki, it reinforces that ESR1-mutant disease should be understood as a recurring resistance state rather than a rare oddity.

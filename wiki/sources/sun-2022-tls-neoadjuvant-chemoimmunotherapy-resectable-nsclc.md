# Maturation and abundance of tertiary lymphoid structures are associated with the efficacy of neoadjuvant chemoimmunotherapy in resectable non-small cell lung cancer

This paper extends TLS biology into the neoadjuvant setting and argues that not just the presence but the maturity and abundance of TLS matter when interpreting response to chemoimmunotherapy in resectable NSCLC.

## Key Points

- Sun et al. examined TLS features in resectable NSCLC treated with neoadjuvant anti-PD-1 plus chemotherapy.
- The paper links greater TLS abundance and more mature TLS organization with better treatment efficacy, positioning TLS maturation as a higher-value biomarker than simple binary presence.
- The study is useful because it shifts TLS biology from metastatic or retrospective checkpoint cohorts into a pathologic-response setting where immune architecture can be compared directly with surgical specimens.
- In the broader wiki, this paper strengthens the idea that organized local immunity is a reusable response-associated pattern across tumor types and treatment contexts.

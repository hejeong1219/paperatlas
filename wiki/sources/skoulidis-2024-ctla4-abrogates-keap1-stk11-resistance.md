# CTLA4 blockade abrogates KEAP1/STK11-related resistance to PD-(L)1 inhibitors

This study revisits one of the hardest checkpoint-resistant lung cancer genotypes and argues that `CTLA-4` co-blockade can partially bypass the `STK11` or `KEAP1` resistance program that limits PD-(L)1 efficacy.

## Key Points

- Skoulidis et al. focus on lung cancers with `KEAP1` and `STK11` alterations, genomic contexts repeatedly associated with poor outcomes on PD-(L)1 monotherapy.
- The central claim is not that the genotype ceases to matter, but that adding CTLA-4 blockade can alter the immune bottleneck enough to recover clinically meaningful activity in this resistant molecular subgroup.
- This is important because it turns a negative biomarker into a treatment-selection problem: the lesion may predict monotherapy failure without predicting universal immunotherapy futility.
- In the wiki's broader logic, this paper serves as a rescue-study counterpart to the 2018 STK11/LKB1 resistance paper, showing how mechanism-informed combinations may be more useful than abandoning immunotherapy outright.
- The work also supports a larger theme running through this corpus: resistance biomarkers are most valuable when they point toward a reversible or bypassable axis.

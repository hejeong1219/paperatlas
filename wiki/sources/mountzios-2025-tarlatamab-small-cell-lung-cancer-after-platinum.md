# Tarlatamab in Small-Cell Lung Cancer after Platinum-Based Chemotherapy

This phase 3 DeLLphi-304 trial shows that DLL3-directed T-cell redirection can outperform standard second-line chemotherapy in small-cell lung cancer, moving tarlatamab from promising single-arm activity into randomized survival benefit.

## Key Points

- Randomized 509 patients with small-cell lung cancer that progressed during or after first-line platinum therapy to tarlatamab or chemotherapy.
- Median overall survival was 13.6 months with tarlatamab versus 8.3 months with chemotherapy, with a hazard ratio for death of 0.60.
- Tarlatamab also improved progression-free survival and patient-reported respiratory symptoms, including dyspnea and cough.
- Grade 3 or higher adverse events were lower with tarlatamab than with chemotherapy (54% vs 80%), and treatment discontinuation due to adverse events was also lower (5% vs 12%).
- In resistance terms, this paper matters because it establishes a new efficacy baseline for DLL3 redirection in SCLC; future relapse papers will need to explain how tumors escape after a regimen that now clearly beats chemotherapy.

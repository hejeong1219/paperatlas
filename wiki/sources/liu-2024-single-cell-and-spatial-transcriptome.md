# Single-cell and spatial transcriptome analyses reveal tertiary lymphoid structures linked to tumour progression and immunotherapy response in nasopharyngeal carcinoma

Single-cell and spatial transcriptomics in nasopharyngeal carcinoma map the cellular architecture of TLS and connect plasma-cell maturation to tumor control and immunotherapy response.

## Key Points

- Analyzes hundreds of thousands of cells and spatial transcriptomic spots from human nasopharyngeal carcinoma samples.
- Identifies CXCL13-positive fibroblasts, stem-like CXCL13-positive CD8 T cells, B cells, and T follicular helper cells as core TLS components.
- Shows that germinal-center reactions mature plasma cells that intersperse with tumor aggregates.
- Links TLS-related cell programs with prognosis and PD-1 blockade response.

## Relevance

- One of the strongest human cancer papers for understanding the spatial and cellular mechanism linking B cells, plasma cells, TLS, and therapy response.
- Useful for any future wiki synthesis that tries to connect antigen burden, local immune organization, and checkpoint sensitivity.

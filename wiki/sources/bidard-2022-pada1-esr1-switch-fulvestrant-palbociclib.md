# Switch to fulvestrant and palbociclib versus no switch in advanced breast cancer with rising ESR1 mutation during aromatase inhibitor and palbociclib therapy (PADA-1): a randomised, open-label, multicentre, phase 3 trial

PADA-1 is a key translational trial because it shows `ESR1` mutation is not only a resistance marker but a decision trigger: ctDNA-detected molecular escape can justify switching endocrine backbone before overt radiographic progression.

## Key Points

- The trial monitored `ESR1` mutations in ctDNA during aromatase inhibitor plus palbociclib therapy and randomized patients with rising ESR1 mutation before clinical progression.
- Switching to `fulvestrant + palbociclib` improved progression-free survival versus staying on the original aromatase inhibitor plus palbociclib.
- The paper matters because it operationalizes molecular resistance surveillance as an interventional strategy rather than a retrospective prognostic exercise.
- It also validates ESR1 mutation as a clinically actionable evolutionary event in endocrine-resistant metastatic breast cancer.
- In this wiki, PADA-1 is the clearest bridge from ESR1 mutation discovery to management change.

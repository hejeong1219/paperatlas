# Overcoming mutation-based resistance to antiandrogens with rational drug design

This paper is a classic mechanism-to-molecule study: it identifies an androgen-receptor mutation that converts enzalutamide from antagonist to agonist and then uses structural reasoning to design compounds that can suppress the resistant receptor.

## Key Points

- Balbas et al. performed mutagenesis screening to identify `AR F876L` as a resistance mutation that can convert enzalutamide into an agonist.
- The study showed that this mutant receptor rescued prostate-cancer cell growth under enzalutamide pressure.
- Structural modeling of the AR-drug complex then guided a focused chemical strategy that produced compounds able to antagonize both wild-type and mutant AR.
- The paper matters because it offers a prototype for how to respond to on-target acquired resistance without abandoning the pathway itself.
- In this wiki, it anchors the prostate-resistance section in the same way T790M or ESR1 hotspot papers anchor other therapy-resistance stories.

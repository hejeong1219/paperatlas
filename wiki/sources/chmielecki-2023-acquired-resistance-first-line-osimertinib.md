# Candidate mechanisms of acquired resistance to first-line osimertinib in EGFR-mutated advanced non-small cell lung cancer

This FLAURA resistance analysis shows that first-line osimertinib failure is only partly explained by recurrent genomic lesions, leaving a large space for non-genetic resistance and microenvironmental remodeling.

## Key Points

- Analyzed paired plasma samples from the phase 3 FLAURA trial to define resistance after first-line osimertinib.
- No acquired EGFR T790M-mediated resistance was detected, separating first-line osimertinib from the classic resistance pattern after earlier-generation EGFR inhibitors.
- The most common candidate mechanisms were MET amplification (16%) and EGFR C797S mutation (6%).
- Fully 65% of patients had no detectable candidate genomic resistance mechanism in plasma at progression.
- The main implication is negative but important: genomic escape exists, yet a majority of first-line resistance is likely driven by biology not captured by recurrent ctDNA alterations alone, including lineage state change, bypass adaptation, or microenvironmental rewiring.

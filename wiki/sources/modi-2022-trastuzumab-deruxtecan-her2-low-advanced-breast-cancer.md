# Trastuzumab Deruxtecan in Previously Treated HER2-Low Advanced Breast Cancer

DESTINY-Breast04 is the pivotal paper that turned HER2-low disease from a descriptive pathology label into a therapeutically actionable state, while also foregrounding interstitial lung disease and target heterogeneity as central tradeoffs.

## Key Points

- Randomized 557 patients with previously treated HER2-low metastatic breast cancer to trastuzumab deruxtecan or physician's-choice chemotherapy.
- In the hormone receptor-positive cohort, median progression-free survival improved from 5.4 to 10.1 months and overall survival from 17.5 to 23.9 months.
- Across all patients, median progression-free survival was 9.9 months versus 5.1 months and overall survival 23.4 months versus 16.8 months.
- Grade 3 or higher adverse events were less common with trastuzumab deruxtecan than with chemotherapy, but adjudicated interstitial lung disease or pneumonitis still occurred in 12.1% of treated patients, including fatal events.
- For resistance biology, the trial implies that relatively low HER2 abundance can still be enough for benefit, but it also makes later HER2 loss, target downregulation, or altered binding especially important escape routes.

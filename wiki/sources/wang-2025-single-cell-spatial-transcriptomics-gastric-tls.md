# Single-cell and spatial transcriptomics implicate a prognostic function of tertiary lymphoid structures in gastric cancer

Human gastric cancer study using single-cell and spatial transcriptomics to show that TLS spatial organization and density have prognostic importance in the tumor microenvironment.

## Key Points

- Uses single-cell and spatial transcriptomics to study TLS architecture in human gastric cancer.
- Frames TLS as a prognostic and immunotherapy-relevant structure whose clinical significance depends on spatial distribution and density rather than simple presence alone.
- Adds gastric cancer to the human TLS atlas, extending the tumor-type range beyond ovarian, nasopharyngeal, and head and neck settings already in the wiki.
- Reinforces the idea that B-cell and TLS biology should be interpreted in spatially organized tumor niches rather than as bulk immune abundance alone.

## Relevance

- Directly relevant to the B-cell neoantigen proposal because gastric cancer is one of the proposal-adjacent tumor contexts already identified as high-value for TLS-rich immune mapping.
- Useful for future syntheses that try to connect antigen discovery with organized lymphoid structure, prognosis, and likely immunotherapy responsiveness.
- Strengthens the B-cell side of the corpus with another recent human-cancer spatial TLS paper in a preferred venue.

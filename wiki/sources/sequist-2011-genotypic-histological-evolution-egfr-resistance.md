# Genotypic and Histological Evolution of Lung Cancers Acquiring Resistance to EGFR Inhibitors

This is a landmark re-biopsy paper because it showed early that acquired resistance to EGFR inhibitors is not one thing: resistant tumors can remain genetically continuous with the original adenocarcinoma, change signaling circuitry, or even switch histology into small-cell lung cancer.

## Key Points

- Sequist et al. analyzed resistant EGFR-mutant NSCLC biopsies and confirmed that the founding activating EGFR mutation was retained even after resistance emerged.
- Known resistance mechanisms such as `EGFR T790M` and `MET` amplification were found, but the study also uncovered less expected changes including `PIK3CA` mutations, EGFR amplification, EMT, and small-cell transformation.
- The small-cell transition finding is especially durable in the field because it established histologic plasticity as a clinically actionable resistance route, not merely a pathologic curiosity.
- Serial biopsies in some patients showed that certain resistance mechanisms could disappear after stopping EGFR inhibitor pressure, supporting the idea that resistance architecture can be dynamic rather than permanently fixed.
- In this wiki, the paper is a foundational bridge between targeted-therapy resistance and lineage-state evolution under treatment pressure.

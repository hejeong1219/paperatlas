# Personalized, autologous neoantigen-specific T cell therapy in metastatic melanoma: a phase 1 trial

This phase 1 study evaluates BNT221, a personalized autologous neoantigen-specific T cell product, in heavily pretreated advanced melanoma.

## Key Points

- Generates individualized neoantigen-specific T cell products from peripheral blood.
- Reports tolerability across tested dose levels without dose-limiting toxicities or major cytokine-release-like syndromes.
- Shows stable disease as the best response in most patients, with tumor shrinkage in a subset.
- Demonstrates polyfunctional mutant-specific CD4 and CD8 responses and persistence of neoantigen-specific clonotypes in blood and tumor.

## Relevance

- Extends the neoantigen pipeline beyond vaccines into adoptive cellular therapy.
- Useful for understanding how candidate neoantigens can be translated into patient-specific cell products.

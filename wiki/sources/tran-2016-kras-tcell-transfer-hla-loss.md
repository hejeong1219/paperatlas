# T-Cell Transfer Therapy Targeting Mutant KRAS in Cancer

This case report is famous for proving that a common driver mutation can be therapeutically targeted by T cells, but it is just as important as a clean demonstration of immune escape through loss of the relevant HLA restriction element.

## Key Points

- A patient with metastatic colorectal cancer received a tumor-infiltrating lymphocyte product enriched for `KRAS G12D`-reactive, `HLA-C*08:02`-restricted CD8 T cells.
- The infused product contained approximately `1.11 x 10^11` KRAS-reactive cells within a total cell dose of approximately `1.48 x 10^11`.
- All seven lung metastases initially regressed, and the patient achieved a partial response lasting about 9 months.
- A progressing lesion retained the `KRAS G12D` mutation but lost the chromosome 6 haplotype encoding `HLA-C*08:02`, demonstrating escape by removing the presentation context rather than the antigen itself.
- The paper is therefore a bridge between neoantigen-targeting optimism and the broader resistance literature on antigen-presentation loss.

# Dissecting the clinicopathologic, genomic, and immunophenotypic correlates of KRAS(G12D)-mutated non-small-cell lung cancer

This paper matters because it argues that `KRAS` is not a single immunologic category in lung cancer: the `G12D` subtype has distinct clinical, genomic, and immune features and appears to fare worse on PD-(L)1 blockade than other KRAS-mutant subgroups.

## Key Points

- Ricciuti et al. compiled a large multi-institutional dataset of KRAS-mutant NSCLC and showed that `KRAS G12D` accounts for a meaningful minority with a distinct clinical profile, including more low or never smokers.
- Compared with non-G12D KRAS-mutant tumors, KRAS G12D cancers displayed different co-mutation patterns and distinct immunophenotypic features.
- The study concludes that KRAS G12D lung cancers have worse outcomes on PD-(L)1 blockade, which is important because it challenges the loose assumption that all KRAS-mutant tumors are relatively immunotherapy-favorable.
- In this wiki, the paper sharpens subtype-level interpretation inside KRAS-mutant NSCLC and complements STK11 or KEAP1-focused resistance work.
- It also provides context for future allele-specific KRAS G12D drug development, which will likely need to account for both oncogenic signaling and immune phenotype.

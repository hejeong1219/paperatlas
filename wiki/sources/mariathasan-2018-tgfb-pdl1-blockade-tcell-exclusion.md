# TGFβ attenuates tumour response to PD-L1 blockade by contributing to exclusion of T cells

Mariathasan et al. is the classic paper on immune exclusion: it shows that failure of checkpoint blockade can reflect not absence of immunity in principle, but physical and stromal sequestration of T cells away from tumor nests.

## Key Points

- The study analyzed metastatic urothelial cancer samples from patients treated with atezolizumab and integrated immune phenotype, transcriptomics, and mutation burden.
- Response correlated with CD8 T-effector signatures and high neoantigen or tumor mutation burden.
- Nonresponse was strongly associated with fibroblast-linked `TGF-beta` signaling, especially in tumors with an immune-excluded phenotype where CD8 T cells accumulated in peritumoral stroma rather than entering tumor parenchyma.
- In a mouse model that recapitulated this excluded state, combined `TGF-beta` blockade plus anti-PD-L1 treatment enabled T-cell penetration and drove tumor regression better than either alone.
- The paper remains central because it reframes some checkpoint resistance as a stromal-architecture problem rather than only a tumor-cell genotype problem.

# Atezolizumab, bevacizumab, pemetrexed and platinum for EGFR-mutant NSCLC patients after EGFR TKI failure: A phase II study with immune cell profile analysis

This phase 2 study is useful because it tries to explain not only whether the IMpower150-like strategy can work after EGFR TKI failure, but also what immune profiles may separate benefit from non-benefit inside this difficult subgroup.

## Key Points

- Wu et al. evaluated a modified regimen of atezolizumab, bevacizumab, pemetrexed, and platinum in EGFR-mutant NSCLC after TKI failure.
- The paper explicitly builds on IMpower150, asking whether an immune-chemotherapy-bevacizumab backbone can salvage patients who remain poorly served by checkpoint therapy alone.
- Re-biopsy tissue and serial blood profiling make the study valuable beyond response rate, because it links outcome to evolving immune context rather than to regimen alone.
- In this wiki, the paper helps move the EGFR-mutant NSCLC discussion from broad pessimism toward conditional salvage strategies with embedded biomarker work.
- It also reinforces that EGFR-driven lung cancer should be studied as a separate immune ecosystem rather than a standard NSCLC subgroup.

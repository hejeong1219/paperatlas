# IL-33-activated ILC2s induce tertiary lymphoid structures in pancreatic cancer

High-impact human pancreatic cancer TLS paper showing that IL-33-associated lymphoneogenic programs and TLS-linked immune organization correlate with better prognosis and can be therapeutically engaged.

## Key Points

- Identifies an IL-33 and ILC2-centered pathway that promotes tertiary lymphoid structure formation in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma.
- Shows human PDAC contains IL-33-expressing cells and putative lymphoneogenic ILC2s within TLS niches, with favorable prognostic association.
- Connects a concrete inflammatory pathway to the emergence of TLS rather than treating TLS as a passive pathology feature.
- Positions TLS induction as a druggable immunotherapy strategy in an otherwise immune-cold disease context.
- Bridges mechanistic lymphoid-structure biology with translational pancreatic cancer immunotherapy.

## Relevance

- Strong direct fit for the B-cell neoantigen proposal because pancreatic cancer was already one of the priority tumor contexts for TLS-rich immune mapping.
- Useful for thinking about how antigen-focused strategies may depend on or benefit from active TLS induction rather than only existing immune structure.
- Expands the B-cell/TLS corpus beyond descriptive spatial studies into a mechanism-linked therapeutic paper in a top-tier venue.

# Tarlatamab for Patients with Previously Treated Small-Cell Lung Cancer

This phase 2 DeLLphi-301 paper is an important proof that T-cell redirection can generate meaningful activity in a solid tumor with few durable post-platinum options.

## Key Points

- Evaluated DLL3-CD3 bispecific tarlatamab in 220 patients with previously treated small-cell lung cancer.
- Reported objective response rates of 40% in the 10-mg cohort and 32% in the 100-mg cohort.
- Responses were not merely brief tumor shrinkages: among responders, 59% had duration of response of at least 6 months.
- Estimated 9-month overall survival in the search-accessible report was 68% for the 10-mg cohort, helping establish clinical credibility for the platform in SCLC.
- Reinforces DLL3 as a tractable lineage-linked target while raising future questions about subtype switching and post-treatment resistance.

# Tumour microenvironment changes after osimertinib treatment resistance in non-small cell lung cancer

This paper complements genomic resistance studies by showing that osimertinib-resistant NSCLC is not merely mutation-shifted but immunologically remodeled toward lower T-cell infiltration and more suppressive myeloid signaling.

## Key Points

- Reports that osimertinib-resistant tumors showed fewer infiltrating T cells and increased M2-like macrophage polarization.
- Finds that exosomes derived from resistant cells can promote M2-polarized macrophage phenotypes, implying an active tumor-driven rewiring process rather than passive immune loss.
- Shows that pre-activated peripheral blood mononuclear cells retained stronger killing activity against resistant cells, suggesting that immune suppression is not absolute and may still be therapeutically reversible.
- Fits the broader pattern in EGFR-mutant lung cancer in which targeted-therapy resistance and immune-cold biology co-evolve.
- Together with ctDNA-based resistance papers, this work argues that post-osimertinib management cannot rely on genomic sequencing alone if the goal is to understand why checkpoint or combination strategies fail.

# Allele-Specific HLA Loss and Immune Escape in Lung Cancer Evolution

McGranahan et al. move the field from single-gene anecdotes to evolutionary architecture, showing that allele-specific HLA loss is common, subclonal, and selectively enriched in settings where immune pressure should matter most.

## Key Points

- The paper introduced LOHHLA, a method for inferring allele-specific HLA copy-number loss from tumor sequencing data.
- HLA loss of heterozygosity was observed in roughly 40% of early-stage non-small-cell lung cancers in the analyzed cohorts.
- HLA loss correlated with higher subclonal neoantigen burden, APOBEC mutagenesis, cytolytic activity, and PD-L1 positivity, which supports the interpretation that immune pressure helps select it.
- The lesion was often subclonal, spatially heterogeneous, and enriched in metastatic samples, making it an evolutionary rather than merely static biomarker.
- Conceptually, the study explains how a tumor can remain immunogenic enough to generate pressure while escaping by deleting only the HLA alleles most relevant for presentation.

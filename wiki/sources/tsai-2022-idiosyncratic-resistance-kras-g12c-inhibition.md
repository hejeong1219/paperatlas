# Rapid idiosyncratic mechanisms of clinical resistance to KRAS G12C inhibition

This study reinforces how individualized KRAS G12C resistance can be in patients: instead of one canonical recurrent lesion, resistant disease may emerge through a mixed package of clonal selection, KRAS-independent signaling, and immune or stromal rewiring.

## Key Points

- Tsai et al. present a rapid-autopsy analysis of KRAS G12C-mutant lung adenocarcinoma after initial response and subsequent resistance to KRAS G12C inhibition.
- Resistant tumors showed decreased `KRAS G12C` allele frequency, MAPK reactivation without a new obvious KRAS-pathway mutation, and several signatures consistent with immune evasion and microenvironment remodeling.
- The paper is useful because it makes clinical resistance look messy in a biologically meaningful way rather than merely unresolved.
- It complements Xue et al. by showing how early adaptive heterogeneity can mature into clinically resistant, site-specific, and idiosyncratic endpoints.
- In this wiki, the study belongs in the same conceptual cluster as other papers arguing that targeted-therapy resistance should be read as ecosystem evolution, not just as mutation replacement.

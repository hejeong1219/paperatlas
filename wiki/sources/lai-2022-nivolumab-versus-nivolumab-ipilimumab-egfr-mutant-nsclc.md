# A Randomized Phase 2 Trial of Nivolumab Versus Nivolumab-Ipilimumab Combination in EGFR-Mutant NSCLC

This trial is valuable precisely because it is negative: it shows that even adding `CTLA-4` blockade to PD-1 inhibition does not meaningfully rescue immune-checkpoint efficacy in unselected EGFR TKI-resistant NSCLC.

## Key Points

- Lai et al. randomized patients with EGFR-mutant, TKI-resistant NSCLC to nivolumab alone or nivolumab plus ipilimumab.
- Recruitment stopped early for futility, with only one objective response across the full cohort and a median progression-free survival of just over one month.
- Neither `PD-L1` nor `TMB` successfully identified a clearly responsive subgroup in this trial.
- This paper matters in the wiki because it provides a prospective clinical anchor for the longstanding impression that EGFR-mutant NSCLC remains poorly served by checkpoint blockade.
- It also tempers enthusiasm for simply transplanting successful immunotherapy combinations from smoking-associated NSCLC into oncogene-driven disease without a stronger mechanistic selection strategy.

# Genetic mechanisms of target antigen loss in CAR19 therapy of acute lymphoblastic leukemia

This brief communication sharpens CD19-negative relapse into a clonally dominant genomic event, emphasizing irreversible structural target loss rather than only altered isoform usage.

## Key Points

- Analyzes relapse samples from CTL019-treated B-ALL and finds CD19 mutations in all assessed CD19-negative relapses.
- Locates mutations across exons 2-5, with many predicted to truncate the protein and eliminate a functional transmembrane domain.
- Shows that mutant allele frequencies track closely with the fraction of CD19-negative leukemic cells, implying that most resistant tumor cells carry the lesion.
- Finds no similar relapse-linked mutation pattern across other tested B-cell marker genes, supporting CD19-specific selection under therapy.
- Strengthens the case for alternative targeting or combination targeting because fully CD19-null relapse can be genetically fixed.

# Personalized neoantigen vaccine and pembrolizumab in advanced hepatocellular carcinoma: a phase 1/2 trial

An open-label phase 1/2 study showing that a personalized DNA neoantigen vaccine plus pembrolizumab can induce vaccine-specific T cell responses and clinical activity in advanced hepatocellular carcinoma.

## Key Points

- Uses a personalized therapeutic cancer vaccine encoding up to 40 neoantigens plus plasmid IL-12 and pembrolizumab.
- Reports manageable safety and no treatment-related grade 3 or higher toxicities.
- Observes objective responses, including complete responses, in advanced HCC.
- Links clinical response with the number of encoded neoantigens and documents neoantigen-specific CD4 and CD8 responses, clonal expansion, and tumor infiltration.

## Relevance

- Strong translational reference for personalized neoantigen therapies in a human cancer setting with real clinical outcomes.
- Useful for pipeline design because it ties sequencing, vaccine manufacturing, immunogenicity assays, and response data together.

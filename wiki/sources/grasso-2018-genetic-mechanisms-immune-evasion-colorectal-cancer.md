# Genetic Mechanisms of Immune Evasion in Colorectal Cancer

This large multi-omic colorectal cancer study is one of the best examples of how immunogenic tumors can still become immune-evasive through hard genetic lesions in antigen presentation, while immune-cold tumors instead rely more on exclusion programs such as WNT activation.

## Key Points

- Integrated genomic data from 1,211 primary colorectal cancers, including 179 MSI-high cases.
- MSI-high tumors showed frequent immune-editing lesions in antigen-presentation machinery, including biallelic losses of B2M and HLA genes through copy-number loss and copy-neutral loss of heterozygosity.
- WNT/beta-catenin pathway genes were significantly altered across colorectal cancer subtypes, and activated WNT signaling correlated with reduced T-cell infiltration.
- The paper separates two broad immune-evasion architectures: antigen-presentation failure in hypermutated, immune-infiltrated tumors versus T-cell exclusion in more common immune-poor tumors.
- It remains a useful conceptual anchor for why MSI-high disease can respond to checkpoint blockade yet still evolve immune escape, whereas most MSS disease starts from a fundamentally non-inflamed state.

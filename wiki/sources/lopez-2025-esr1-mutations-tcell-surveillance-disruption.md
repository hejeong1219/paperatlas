# Exploring the role of ESR1 mutations in metastatic hormone receptor-positive breast cancer T cell immune surveillance disruption

This paper extends the ESR1 story into immunology by asking whether endocrine resistance in metastatic HR-positive breast cancer also distorts antitumor T-cell surveillance.

## Key Points

- Lopez et al. examine how `ESR1` mutations may contribute not only to ligand-independent ER signaling but also to disrupted T-cell immune surveillance in metastatic HR-positive breast cancer.
- The study is useful because it pushes ESR1-mutant disease beyond a purely endocrine-resistance framework into an immunobiology framework.
- In the context of this wiki, it suggests endocrine resistance might have downstream immune consequences that help maintain metastatic persistence.
- This is a conceptually important move: rather than treating targeted or endocrine resistance and immune escape as separate silos, the paper looks for mechanistic overlap.
- It also gives the breast-cancer section a stronger connection to the broader theme of treatment-induced immune-state remodeling.

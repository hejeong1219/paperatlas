# Convergence of Acquired Mutations and Alternative Splicing of CD19 Enables Resistance to CART-19 Immunotherapy

This study established antigen escape in CD19 CAR-T as a molecular event rather than only a flow-cytometry observation, showing that mutation and splicing can converge on epitope loss.

## Key Points

- Identifies hemizygous deletion, exon 2 mutations, and exon 2 skipping as convergent routes to CD19 epitope loss in relapsed B-ALL after CART-19.
- Shows that alternatively spliced CD19 isoforms can preserve partial CD19 biology while removing recognition by the therapeutic CAR.
- Links reduced `SRSF3` expression to exon 2 skipping, making splicing control part of the resistance mechanism rather than a passive byproduct.
- Supports the idea that antigen escape can preserve leukemic fitness instead of requiring complete target loss.
- Motivates alternative epitope targeting, dual-antigen strategies, and designs less vulnerable to a single splice-dependent failure point.

## Relevance

- This is one of the key mechanistic papers behind the modern antigen-loss literature in CAR-T.
- It pairs naturally with later reports showing irreversible CD19 truncation, lineage switch, and antigen-density escape.

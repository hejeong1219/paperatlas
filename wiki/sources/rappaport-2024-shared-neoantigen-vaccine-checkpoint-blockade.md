# A shared neoantigen vaccine combined with immune checkpoint blockade for advanced metastatic solid tumors: phase 1 trial interim results

This phase 1 trial tests a shared neoantigen vaccine strategy with ipilimumab and nivolumab in advanced metastatic solid tumors selected by mutation and HLA context.

## Key Points

- Evaluates a vaccine encoding 20 shared neoantigens derived from common driver mutations.
- Selects patients by HLA-matched tumor mutations, with most enrolled patients harboring KRAS alterations.
- Combines chimp adenoviral priming and self-amplifying mRNA boosting with checkpoint blockade.
- Reports acceptable safety with mostly grade 1 or 2 treatment-related adverse events.

## Relevance

- Bridges discovery of shared neoepitope-HLA targets with a clinically deployable off-the-shelf style therapeutic strategy.
- Relevant when comparing private versus shared neoantigen pipeline design choices.

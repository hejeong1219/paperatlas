# Trastuzumab Deruxtecan after Endocrine Therapy in Metastatic Breast Cancer

DESTINY-Breast06 pushes trastuzumab deruxtecan earlier in the treatment sequence, showing that HER2-low and even HER2-ultralow metastatic breast cancer can benefit before conventional chemotherapy for metastatic disease.

## Key Points

- Randomized 866 patients with hormone receptor-positive metastatic breast cancer after one or more lines of endocrine-based therapy and before chemotherapy for metastatic disease.
- In the HER2-low population, median progression-free survival was 13.2 months with trastuzumab deruxtecan versus 8.1 months with chemotherapy, with a hazard ratio of 0.62.
- Exploratory results were directionally consistent in the HER2-ultralow subgroup, widening the clinically relevant HER2 spectrum beyond the original HER2-low category.
- Grade 3 or higher adverse events occurred in 52.8% of trastuzumab-deruxtecan recipients, and adjudicated interstitial lung disease or pneumonitis occurred in 11.3%, including three grade 5 events.
- The paper matters for resistance because it weakens the idea of a fixed HER2 threshold; if benefit extends into ultralow disease, then dynamic target expression, binding efficiency, payload sensitivity, and spatial heterogeneity become even more central than a single baseline IHC call.

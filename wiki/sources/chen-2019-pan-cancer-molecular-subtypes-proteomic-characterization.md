# Pan-cancer molecular subtypes revealed by mass-spectrometry-based proteomic characterization of more than 500 human cancers

Landmark CPTAC-centered pan-cancer study showing that proteomic and phosphoproteomic profiling can define cross-lineage molecular subtypes not fully captured by transcriptomics.

## Key Points

- Analyzes 532 human cancers across multiple tissue types using mass-spectrometry-based proteomic profiling, with phosphoproteomic features incorporated into subtype characterization.
- Defines ten proteome-based pan-cancer subtypes that cut across tumor lineage and reveal pathway structure missed by transcriptomics alone.
- Identifies immune-related and stromal-related subtypes, including one proteome-defined subtype associated with humoral immune response.
- Shows that pan-cancer proteomic organization can recover clinically and biologically meaningful structure from human tumor cohorts.
- Provides an older but still foundational benchmark for cross-cohort human cancer proteomics and phosphoproteomics.

## Relevance

- Useful for the ptmanchor manuscript as a landmark human-cancer cohort reference showing why protein and phosphoprotein layers deserve interpretation beyond transcript-level summaries.
- Supports the broader framing that abundance-aware protein and PTM analysis can reveal biology that standard RNA-based classification underestimates.
- Also marginally relevant to the B-cell neoantigen side because one subtype centers humoral immunity, but its primary value here is as foundational pan-cancer proteomics background.

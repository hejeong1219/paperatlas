# Blinatumomab versus Chemotherapy for Advanced Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia

This phase 3 study established blinatumomab as a clinically meaningful T-cell-redirecting therapy in relapsed or refractory B-cell precursor ALL, creating the selective landscape in which later CD19 escape studies became important.

## Key Points

- Randomized adults with heavily pretreated Ph-negative B-cell precursor ALL to blinatumomab or standard chemotherapy.
- Improved median overall survival to 7.7 months versus 4.0 months with chemotherapy, with a hazard ratio for death of 0.71.
- Increased remission rates within 12 weeks, including higher rates of MRD-negative remission than chemotherapy.
- Showed that CD19-directed bispecific redirection can outperform salvage chemotherapy in a difficult disease setting, not just induce transient responses in single-arm studies.
- Provides the therapeutic backdrop for later questions about CD19 persistence, antigen escape, and whether sequential CD19-directed therapies intensify selection pressure.

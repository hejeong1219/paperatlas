# Immunomodulatory effects of trastuzumab deruxtecan through the cGAS-STING pathway in gastric cancer cells

This paper is important because it frames `trastuzumab deruxtecan` not only as a delivery system for cytotoxic payload, but also as a driver of innate immune activation through DNA damage and `cGAS-STING` signaling.

## Key Points

- Oh et al. show that `T-DXd` induces DNA damage and apoptosis in gastric cancer cells across different HER2 expression levels.
- Treatment increased `PD-L1` expression and upregulated inflammatory and interferon-related gene programs, suggesting that ADC treatment can reshape tumor immunogenicity.
- The mechanistic centerpiece is activation of the `cGAS-STING` pathway with a type I interferon response in HER2-positive gastric cancer cells.
- In this wiki, the paper matters because it gives a mechanistic rationale for combining HER2-targeted ADCs with checkpoint approaches and complicates the idea that ADCs are purely cell-autonomous drugs.
- It also provides an immune-activation counterpoint to later papers focused on HER2 loss and binding-site escape under T-DXd pressure.

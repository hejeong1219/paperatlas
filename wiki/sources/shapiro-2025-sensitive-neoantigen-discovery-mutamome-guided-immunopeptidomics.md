# Sensitive neoantigen discovery by real-time mutanome-guided immunopeptidomics

Clinical-facing immunopeptidomics paper introducing a more sensitive workflow for personalized neoantigen discovery from scarce tumor material.

## Key Points

- Presents `NeoDiscMS`, a real-time mutanome-guided immunopeptidomics workflow designed for personalized clinical antigen discovery.
- Uses sequencing-guided real-time spectral acquisition to improve sensitivity for low-abundance neoantigen targets while preserving broader immunopeptidome depth.
- Aims explicitly at practical clinical constraints such as limited sample input and short turnaround time from tumor profiling to therapeutic use.
- Reports improved confidence in neoantigen identification compared with standard approaches and increased detection of tumor-associated antigen peptides.
- Positions mass-spectrometry-based immunopeptidomics as a practical refinement layer above purely computational neoantigen prediction.

## Relevance

- Directly relevant to the B-cell neoantigen proposal because it strengthens the immunopeptidomics arm of personalized antigen discovery in human cancer settings.
- Useful for the proposal's pipeline logic where candidate prioritization should be anchored in naturally presented HLA peptides whenever feasible.
- Although still primarily T-cell/HLA centered, it materially improves the upstream discovery machinery that the broader B-cell/TLS program depends on.

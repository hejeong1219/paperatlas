# Zanidatamab in HER2-Positive Metastatic Biliary Tract Cancer: Final Results From HERIZON-BTC-01

This final HERIZON-BTC-01 analysis shows that zanidatamab responses in HER2-positive biliary tract cancer are not only real but often durable, with extended follow-up starting to clarify which HER2-defined subsets benefit most.

## Key Points

- After 33.4 months of median follow-up, confirmed objective response rate remained 41.3% across the 80-patient efficacy set.
- Median duration of response was 14.9 months and median overall survival was 15.5 months, notable numbers for previously treated metastatic biliary tract cancer.
- Benefit was concentrated in IHC 3+ disease, where confirmed objective response rate rose to 51.6% and median overall survival to 18.1 months.
- Pain outcomes tracked with tumor response: responders improved, whereas patients with progressive disease reported worsening pain.
- The follow-up sharpens a resistance-relevant point already visible in the first report: HER2 amplification alone is not enough, because biologic dependence appears much stronger in the IHC 3+ subset than in IHC 2+/amplified disease.

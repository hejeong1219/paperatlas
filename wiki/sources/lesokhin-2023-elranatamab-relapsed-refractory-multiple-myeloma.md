# Elranatamab in relapsed or refractory multiple myeloma: phase 2 MagnetisMM-3 trial results

This phase 2 study shows that BCMA-CD3 redirection can deliver deep responses in triple-class refractory myeloma while also making dose-schedule engineering part of the safety strategy.

## Key Points

- In 123 patients without prior BCMA-directed therapy, elranatamab achieved a 61.0% confirmed objective response rate and 35.0% complete response or better.
- Response durability was encouraging, with median duration of response, progression-free survival, and overall survival not yet reached at 14.7 months.
- Patients switching from weekly to every-two-week dosing after sustained response often maintained or deepened benefit, suggesting schedule adaptation can preserve efficacy while reducing toxicity burden.
- Safety was dominated by infections, anemia, neutropenia, and mostly low-grade cytokine release syndrome.
- Responses were somewhat lower in extramedullary and penta-refractory disease, but activity remained clinically meaningful even in poor-risk subsets.

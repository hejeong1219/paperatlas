# Interferon Epsilon Loss Is Elusive 9p21 Link to Immune-Cold Tumors, Resistant to Immune Checkpoint Therapy, and Endogenous CXCL9/10 Induction

This article argues that the immunologic consequence of 9p loss is not just deletion of famous tumor suppressors but collapse of endogenous interferon-driven chemokine programs that normally help recruit effector immunity.

## Key Points

- Zhao et al. synthesize emerging clinical and experimental evidence tying chromosome `9p21` loss to immune-cold, checkpoint-resistant tumors across several human cancer types.
- The paper emphasizes type I interferon loss, especially `IFN-epsilon`, as a plausible mechanistic bridge between 9p deletion and impaired endogenous `CXCL9/10` induction.
- It helps explain why some tumors with apparently actionable immunotherapy biomarkers still fail to recruit dendritic cells and CD8 T cells effectively.
- The article is most useful as a framework paper: it shifts attention from descriptive "immune desert" language toward a specific chromosome-arm and chemokine-induction model.
- Because this is a review-like synthesis rather than a single cohort paper, it is best used as a map of the emerging 9p immune-cold literature rather than as a standalone proof source.

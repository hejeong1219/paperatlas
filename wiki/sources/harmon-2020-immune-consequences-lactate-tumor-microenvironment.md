# The Immune Consequences of Lactate in the Tumor Microenvironment

This review frames lactate as more than a glycolytic waste product, positioning it instead as a microenvironmental signal that can directly weaken antitumor immunity while reinforcing multiple suppressive cell states.

## Key Points

- Harmon, O'Farrelly, and Robinson synthesize how tumor-derived lactate and lactic acidosis reshape immune behavior inside the tumor microenvironment.
- The core idea is that lactate accumulation is not metabolically neutral: it can impair effector functions in CD8 T cells and NK cells while favoring suppressive programs in myeloid and regulatory compartments.
- The review is conceptually important because it links metabolism to immune exclusion without requiring a purely genetic resistance lesion; a tumor can remain antigenic yet still become immunologically ineffective if the metabolic terrain is hostile.
- In the resistance framework of this wiki, lactate helps explain why some immune-cold or immune-dysfunctional states are maintained by environmental pressure rather than by a single mutational event.
- The paper also points toward therapeutic logic: inhibiting lactate production, export, or acidification could serve as an enabling strategy for immunotherapy rather than only as a tumor-cell-directed metabolic intervention.

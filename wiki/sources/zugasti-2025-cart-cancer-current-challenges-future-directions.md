# CAR-T cell therapy for cancer: current challenges and future directions

This review extends the CAR-T discussion into one of the hardest frontiers, arguing that AML crystallizes many of the unresolved safety and targeting problems that remain after the initial wave of B-cell success.

## Key Points

- Zugasti et al. review CAR-T across hematologic malignancies and solid tumors, but put particular emphasis on `AML` as a stress test for the platform.
- The central AML challenge is the lack of a target that is both effective and safe, because many candidate antigens overlap with healthy hematopoietic stem and progenitor compartments.
- The review therefore spends more time on `on-target/off-tumor toxicity`, prolonged myeloablation risk, gating logic, bispecific strategies, and bone marrow niche suppression than earlier general reviews.
- It also surveys future directions such as safety switches, logic-gated CARs, STAb-like bystander-recruiting strategies, TRUCKs, and microenvironment modulation.
- In this corpus, the paper is valuable less as a catalogue of approved success and more as a map of where CAR-T still breaks.

# Mutations Associated with Acquired Resistance to PD-1 Blockade in Melanoma

This paired-biopsy melanoma study remains a canonical example of acquired checkpoint resistance: tumors that initially regressed under pembrolizumab later escaped through hard lesions in interferon signaling or antigen presentation rather than through simple loss of T-cell infiltration.

## Key Points

- The study examined four metastatic melanoma patients with initial objective regression on pembrolizumab followed by delayed progression despite continued therapy.
- Two resistant tumors acquired truncating loss-of-function alterations in `JAK1` or `JAK2` together with loss of the remaining wild-type allele, functionally abolishing interferon-gamma responsiveness.
- A third resistant tumor acquired a truncating `B2M` lesion with loss of surface MHC class I expression, directly impairing CD8 T-cell recognition.
- The paper is important because resistance emerged after an active immune response had already occurred; this argues for immunoediting and selection rather than baseline immune ignorance.
- Zaretsky et al. also note that one patient lacked an obvious recurrent genomic driver, leaving room for non-genetic resistance programs even in this early landmark series.

# B cells and tertiary lymphoid structures in cancer therapy response

This review synthesizes recent evidence that B cells and TLS are clinically meaningful components of the tumor microenvironment and potential predictors of therapy response across cancers.

## Key Points

- Reviews B-cell phenotypes in the tumor microenvironment, including antitumor and immunoregulatory states.
- Summarizes how TLS maturity and density relate to prognosis and response to immune checkpoint blockade.
- Emphasizes that most of the field has been T-cell-centric and that B-cell roles are only recently being integrated.
- Highlights pathology-facing opportunities to quantify B cells and TLS as practical biomarkers.

## Relevance

- Serves as a high-level orientation page connecting the more specific ovarian, NPC, and HNSCC studies in this corpus.
- Helpful as a synthesis source when the wiki needs a broad framing of the B-cell/TLS literature.

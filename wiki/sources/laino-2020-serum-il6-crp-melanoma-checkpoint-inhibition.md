# Serum interleukin-6 and C-reactive protein are associated with survival in melanoma patients receiving immune checkpoint inhibition

This biomarker paper is useful because it shows that checkpoint outcome is shaped not only by tumor-local features but also by systemic inflammatory state, with IL-6 standing out as a robust adverse prognostic signal.

## Key Points

- Laino et al. analyzed serum from three randomized melanoma trials, including CheckMate-064, CheckMate-066, and CheckMate-067.
- Higher baseline `IL-6` and neutrophil-to-lymphocyte ratio, and to a lesser degree `CRP`, were associated with shorter overall survival in patients receiving immune checkpoint inhibitors or chemotherapy.
- Increased on-treatment `IL-6` in CheckMate-064 also tracked with worse survival, suggesting the signal is not purely baseline noise.
- In multivariable analysis of CheckMate-067, `IL-6` remained a significant prognostic factor, which is why the paper still gets cited more often than a generic serum-correlate study.
- Conceptually, the study links circulating inflammation to immunotherapy outcome and creates a bridge between tumor microenvironment biology and whole-body inflammatory tone.

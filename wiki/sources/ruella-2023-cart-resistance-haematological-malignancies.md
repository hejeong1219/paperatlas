# Mechanisms of resistance to chimeric antigen receptor-T cells in haematological malignancies

This review is one of the most complete organizing maps of CAR-T failure, because it treats resistance as a three-part problem involving the cell product, the tumor, and the surrounding microenvironment.

## Key Points

- Ruella et al. divide CAR-T resistance into `CAR-T cell dysfunction`, `tumour-intrinsic resistance`, and `immunosuppressive tumour microenvironment` failure modes.
- The review distinguishes primary resistance from relapse after initial response and notes that resistance remains common across B-ALL, DLBCL, mantle cell lymphoma, follicular lymphoma, and multiple myeloma.
- Beyond antigen-negative relapse, the paper emphasizes accessory-ligand loss, impaired apoptotic susceptibility, lineage plasticity, and microenvironmental suppression.
- It is especially helpful for design logic because it links specific failure modes to engineering responses such as multi-target constructs, armoured CARs, signaling optimization, and rational combination therapy.
- In this corpus, Ruella is the broad synthesis that helps contextualize the more platform-level but slightly older Sterner review and the forward-looking Zugasti review.

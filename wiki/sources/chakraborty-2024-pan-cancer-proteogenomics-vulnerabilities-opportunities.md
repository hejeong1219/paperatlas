# Integrate and conquer: pan-cancer proteogenomics uncovers cancer vulnerabilities and therapeutic opportunities

Short high-impact review highlighting how integrated CPTAC-style pan-cancer proteogenomics can reveal targetable dependencies, neoantigens, and therapeutic opportunities missed by single-layer analyses.

## Key Points

- Summarizes recent pan-cancer proteogenomic integration across CPTAC and related public datasets.
- Emphasizes discrepancies between mRNA abundance and protein abundance, reinforcing the importance of direct proteomic measurement.
- Highlights how proteomics and phosphoproteomics can expose overexpressed targets, activated kinases, and lineage-specific vulnerabilities.
- Frames multi-omics integration as a practical route to drug discovery, target prioritization, and antigen nomination across cancers.

## Relevance

- Useful for the ptmanchor manuscript as a concise review-level justification for protein- and phosphoprotein-aware cancer analysis.
- Serves as a bridge between the landmark primary proteogenomic papers already in the wiki and a broader therapeutic interpretation layer.
- Less central than primary cohort papers, but still helpful background for why proteogenomics matters clinically and computationally.

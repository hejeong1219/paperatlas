# Tumor Mutation Burden and Efficacy of EGFR-Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitors in Patients with EGFR-Mutant Lung Cancers

This paper is useful because it turns `TMB` into more than an immunotherapy biomarker: in EGFR-mutant NSCLC, higher TMB marked worse outcomes on EGFR TKIs, suggesting biological complexity that is unfavorable for both targeted therapy durability and later immune responsiveness.

## Key Points

- Offin et al. analyzed EGFR exon 19 deletion or L858R mutant metastatic lung cancers with pretreatment broad-panel sequencing and related baseline `TMB` to clinical outcomes on first- and second-generation EGFR TKIs.
- EGFR-mutant tumors had lower TMB overall than EGFR wild-type lung adenocarcinomas, but the high-TMB subset within EGFR-mutant disease showed shorter time to treatment discontinuation and shorter overall survival.
- Paired samples also suggested that TMB can rise at resistance, reinforcing the idea that the genome evolves toward greater complexity under treatment pressure.
- In the wiki's framework, this paper helps explain why EGFR-mutant NSCLC is not a simple low-TMB monolith and why a more mutation-rich subset may paradoxically behave worse with targeted therapy.
- It also complements later checkpoint studies showing that neither PD-L1 nor TMB alone reliably rescues patient selection in EGFR-driven disease.

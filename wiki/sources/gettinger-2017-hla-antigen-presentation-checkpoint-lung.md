# Impaired HLA Class I Antigen Processing and Presentation as a Mechanism of Acquired Resistance to Immune Checkpoint Inhibitors in Lung Cancer

This lung cancer resistance study shows that checkpoint escape in thoracic tumors can converge on the same antigen-presentation axis seen in melanoma, even if the exact genomic events are heterogeneous.

## Key Points

- Gettinger et al. analyzed 14 immune-checkpoint-resistant lung cancer specimens and found no single dominant recurrent lesion across antigen-presentation or interferon pathways.
- One patient developed acquired homozygous `B2M` loss with absent HLA class I expression in both tumor and matched patient-derived xenograft material.
- Two additional patient-derived xenografts showed `B2M` downregulation, supporting the idea that full genetic knockout is not the only way the pathway can fail.
- In an immunocompetent mouse lung cancer model, CRISPR `B2m` knockout conferred resistance to PD-1 blockade, giving functional support to the human observations.
- The paper is especially useful because it argues against searching for a single universal resistance genotype while still preserving a coherent mechanistic axis: loss of class I antigen display.

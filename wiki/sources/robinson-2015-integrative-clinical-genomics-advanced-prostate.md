# Integrative clinical genomics of advanced prostate cancer

This is a foundational precision-oncology paper for metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer because it showed that prospective comprehensive sequencing is feasible and clinically relevant in a disease long treated as androgen-axis dominant but genomically under-profiled.

## Key Points

- Robinson et al. profiled metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer using prospective whole-exome and transcriptome sequencing from soft-tissue or bone biopsies.
- `AR`, `ETS`, `TP53`, and `PTEN` pathway alterations were frequent, with `AR` and `TP53` more enriched in advanced disease than in primary prostate cancer.
- The study also identified actionable alterations in DNA repair genes such as `BRCA2`, `BRCA1`, and `ATM` at higher frequencies than expected from primary disease.
- The paper matters because it reframed advanced prostate cancer as molecularly diverse yet often actionable, rather than as a disease explained almost entirely by androgen dependence.
- In this wiki, it serves as a backbone resource for later prostate-resistance papers focused on AR alteration, splice variants, and immune context.

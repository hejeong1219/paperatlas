# Tertiary lymphoid structures and B cells determine clinically relevant T cell phenotypes in ovarian cancer

This ovarian cancer study shows that mature TLS and associated B-cell states shape the phenotype and functional quality of intratumoral T cells.

## Key Points

- Profiles TLS organization and maturation in high-grade serous ovarian carcinoma.
- Finds mature TLS in a minority of tumors with relatively higher mutational burden.
- Links mature TLS to increased intratumoral CD8 effector cells and distinctive checkpoint-associated T cell states.
- Shows that B-cell depletion shifts tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes toward more dysfunctional CD8 phenotypes in ex vivo assays.

## Relevance

- Directly relevant to the B-cell half of a B-cell/neoantigen pipeline because it connects B-cell organization to the downstream quality of antitumor T-cell responses.
- Suggests that antigen discovery alone is insufficient without understanding the local TLS and B-cell context.

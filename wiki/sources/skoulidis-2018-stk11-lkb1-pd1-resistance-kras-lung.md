# STK11/LKB1 Mutations and PD-1 Inhibitor Resistance in KRAS-Mutant Lung Adenocarcinoma

This paper established `STK11/LKB1` loss as one of the clearest genomic correlates of primary resistance to PD-1 pathway blockade in KRAS-mutant lung adenocarcinoma, helping define a clinically important immune-cold molecular subgroup.

## Key Points

- Skoulidis et al. analyzed KRAS-mutant lung adenocarcinoma and showed markedly inferior response and survival outcomes with PD-1 axis blockade in tumors harboring co-occurring `STK11/LKB1` alterations.
- The mechanistic framing is not just genotype-outcome correlation; the paper links this subgroup to a non-T-cell-inflamed microenvironment with reduced immune engagement.
- The study became influential because it moved the field beyond PD-L1-only thinking and suggested that tumor suppressor context can preconfigure whether checkpoint therapy has any immune substrate to act on.
- In the resistance architecture of this wiki, `STK11/LKB1` is a canonical example of a tumor-intrinsic lesion that expresses itself as a microenvironmental failure state rather than as a simple antigen-presentation defect.
- The paper also set up the later question of whether combination immunotherapy or alternative immune-priming approaches can specifically rescue this subgroup.

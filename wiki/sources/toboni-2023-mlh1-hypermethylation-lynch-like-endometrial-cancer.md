# Differential outcomes and immune checkpoint inhibitor response among endometrial cancer patients with MLH1 hypermethylation versus MLH1 "Lynch-like" mismatch repair gene mutation

This paper is a strong reminder that mismatch-repair-deficient endometrial cancers are not a single immune class: the route by which MLH1 is lost appears to shape both immune tone and clinical outcome.

## Key Points

- Toboni et al. profiled 1,673 mismatch-repair-deficient endometrial tumors and compared `MLH1` hypermethylated disease with `MLH1`-mutated, "Lynch-like" tumors.
- The `MLH1` hypermethylated group was older and had significantly worse overall survival, with a trend toward worse outcome after immune-checkpoint inhibitor treatment.
- Relative to the Lynch-like group, hypermethylated tumors showed lower PD-L1 positivity, lower immune-checkpoint gene expression, lower immune-cell infiltration, and lower T-cell-inflamed and interferon-gamma scores.
- The authors also report altered co-mutation structure, including increased `JAK1`, `FGFR2`, `CCND1`, and `PTEN` mutation rates plus higher ER/PR positivity in the hypermethylated cohort.
- The practical implication is not that dMMR stops mattering, but that the same biomarker label can hide clinically meaningful immune-cold subgroups.

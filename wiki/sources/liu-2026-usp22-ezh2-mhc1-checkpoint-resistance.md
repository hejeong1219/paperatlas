# USP22 drives tumor immune evasion and checkpoint blockade resistance through EZH2-mediated epigenetic silencing of MHC-I

This study adds an important reversible layer to the antigen-presentation story by showing that checkpoint resistance can arise from epigenetic repression of MHC-I rather than only from irreversible structural loss.

## Key Points

- Liu et al. identify `USP22` as a tumor-intrinsic suppressor of MHC-I-dependent neoantigen presentation and CD8 T-cell recognition.
- Mechanistically, USP22 deubiquitinates and stabilizes `EZH2`, which then represses MHC-I pathway genes transcriptionally.
- Genetic or pharmacologic USP22 inhibition restored tumor immunogenicity and improved anti-PD-1 sensitivity in preclinical systems.
- The study also links higher USP22 expression with lower `B2M` levels and reduced CD8 infiltration across solid tumors, reinforcing the clinical relevance of this axis.
- This paper matters because it widens the intervention space: not every antigen-presentation defect is a hard deletion, and some may be pharmacologically reversible.

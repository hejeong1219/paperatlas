# Systematic discovery of neoepitope-HLA pairs for neoantigens shared among patients and tumor types

This paper describes a scalable experimental and computational platform for identifying validated shared neoepitope-HLA pairs, moving neoantigen targeting beyond fully private targets.

## Key Points

- Couples peptide-HLA binding assays, engineered monoallelic cell models, and mass spectrometry.
- Screens more than 24,000 candidate neoepitope-HLA combinations across recurrent cancer mutations and common HLA alleles.
- Narrows to hundreds of stable candidates and validates dozens of presented neoepitope-HLA pairs.
- Demonstrates downstream immunogenicity by identifying TCRs recognizing selected validated pairs.

## Relevance

- Important for any pipeline that wants to support both personalized and shared neoantigen strategies.
- Provides a reference for validation stages after initial in silico candidate generation.

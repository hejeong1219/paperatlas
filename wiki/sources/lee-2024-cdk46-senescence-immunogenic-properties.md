# CDK4/6 inhibitors induce breast cancer senescence with enhanced anti-tumor immunogenic properties compared with DNA-damaging agents

This paper argues that not all therapy-induced senescence is immunologically equal: `CDK4/6` inhibitor-induced senescence may preserve more antitumor immune features than DNA-damaging therapy-induced senescence.

## Key Points

- Lee et al. compared senescence induced by CDK4/6 inhibitors with senescence induced by conventional DNA-damaging agents in breast cancer cells.
- Although both induced senescence, DNA-damaging agents produced more pro-tumorigenic SASP features such as `IL6`, `CXCL8`, `TGFβ`, `CD274`, and `VEGFA`.
- By contrast, CDK4/6 inhibitor-induced senescent cells maintained or strengthened expression of antigen-presentation and T-cell-chemokine programs such as `MHC-I`, `B2M`, and `CXCL9/10/11`.
- In the wiki, this paper is important because it reframes CDK4/6 therapy as a potential immune-context shaper rather than only a cell-cycle brake.
- It also helps explain why senescence can be therapeutically helpful or harmful depending on the specific drug-induced phenotype.

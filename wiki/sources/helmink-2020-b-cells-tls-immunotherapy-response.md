# B cells and tertiary lymphoid structures promote immunotherapy response

Helmink et al. is one of the most important papers in the modern TLS literature because it pushed checkpoint biomarker thinking beyond a T-cell-only frame and made B-cell-rich TLS organization a credible response-associated feature.

## Key Points

- In checkpoint-treated cohorts, B-cell markers were among the strongest differentially expressed features in responders versus non-responders.
- The association was observed across more than one treated cohort, including melanoma and renal cell carcinoma settings.
- Histology localized these B cells within `tertiary lymphoid structures`, arguing that immune organization matters more than simple cell counts alone.
- Additional analyses suggested clonal expansion and functionally distinctive B-cell states, including enrichment of switched memory B cells in responders.
- The paper therefore reframed B cells and TLS from bystanders into candidate biomarkers and possibly active contributors to effective anti-tumor immunity.

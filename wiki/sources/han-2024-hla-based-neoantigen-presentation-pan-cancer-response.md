# Assessment of human leukocyte antigen-based neoantigen presentation to determine pan-cancer response to immunotherapy

Pan-cancer immunotherapy study showing that quantitative assessment of HLA-mediated neoantigen presentation helps predict checkpoint response beyond simple mutation burden alone.

## Key Points

- Develops the `HLA tumor-Antigen Presentation Score (HAPS)` using neoantigen-HLA binding and HLA class I allele divergence.
- Uses a pooled pan-cancer cohort of patients treated with immune checkpoint inhibitors to connect antigen presentation capacity with survival benefit.
- Shows that patients with higher inferred presentation capacity are more likely to benefit from ICI treatment.
- Links stronger presentation capacity with enrichment of antigen-presentation pathways in the tumor microenvironment.
- Extends biomarker logic from raw neoantigen quantity toward presentation-aware and recognition-aware immune modeling.

## Relevance

- Relevant to the B-cell neoantigen proposal because it strengthens the conceptual bridge between neoantigen generation and the immune context in which those antigens can become clinically meaningful.
- Useful as a pan-cancer response-model paper for the proposal's broader biomarker and prioritization logic.
- Still T-cell/HLA centered, but important as a higher-resolution neoantigen presentation framework in human tumors.
